# 🧪 Alchemy

Alchemy is primarily resource management. Many strong potions and elixirs depend on rare ingredients, while common ingredients can serve as flexible filler.

> [!warning] Core habit
> Never let the Any slot consume rare ingredients automatically. Choose filler ingredients manually before crafting.

## ⚠️ Core Rules

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ 💰 🧪

- Never consume rare ingredients uncontrolled in the **Any** slot.
- Always select the second ingredient slot manually.
- Before crafting, check which other recipes still need an ingredient.
- Do not sell ingredients indiscriminately.
- Recheck vendors after a Long Rest.

Frequently mentioned ingredients are summarized in [[15_Appendix#^glossary-ingredients|Glossary: Ingredients Mentioned Often]].

## 🎛️ Control the Any Slot

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ 💰 🧪

In the alchemy window, you can choose which **Any** ingredient is consumed. This control matters because the game can otherwise use valuable ingredients for simple recipes.

Cheap Any supplements:

- Wispweed
- Poison Spores

## 🔎 Check Ingredients Before Crafting

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ 💰 🧪

Hovering over ingredients shows which recipes still need them. Use this before crafting, especially with rare ingredients from bosses, special areas, or later acts.

## 📖 Extract Ingredients and Read Recipes

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⏱️ 🧪

Recipes can come from extracting ingredients and from recipe notes, books, or scrolls found in the world. Extract ingredients regularly instead of letting raw materials sit unused forever.

Alchemy can be used on the road; it is not limited to camp or a special crafting station. This makes mid-adventure potion crafting practical when the party is missing one key consumable.

Additional source videos: [How Alchemy Works in Baldur's Gate 3 - Rob Unwraps](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DzDpduPcINI), [Baldur's Gate 3: Best Potions and Elixirs To Craft - Medieval Marty](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Lqk4yu3dxP8).

## 🛒 Supply Through Vendors

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ 💰 🧪

Plants and monster ingredients do not reliably respawn. Long-term supply mainly comes from vendors and their refreshed inventories after a Long Rest.

Important vendors are listed in [Vendors and Resources](07_Vendors_and_Resources.md).

## ✅ Practical Crafting Order

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ 🧪

1. Select the recipe.
2. Check the main ingredient.
3. Set the Any slot manually.
4. Hover over ingredients to check other uses.
5. Craft only after that.

This order prevents rare ingredients from being spent on low-priority recipes.

## 🧙 Double Important Crafting With Transmutation

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ 💰 🧪 🎯

A Transmutation Wizard can sometimes create extra alchemical output through Experimental Alchemy. This is especially valuable for rare or high-impact consumables.

Practical setup:

1. Use the character with the best Medicine check for important crafting.
2. Add Wisdom, Medicine proficiency, or skill support if available.
3. Craft scarce resources such as speed, strength, resistance, or high-tier healing supplies with that character when possible.

This is an optimization, not a required routine. It matters most when rare ingredients are the limiting resource.

Source video: [Baldur's Gate 3: The Ultimate Buff Stacking Guide - SwoleBenji](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fjS7vvau0o0).
