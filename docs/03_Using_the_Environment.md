# 🏔️ Using the Environment

The environment is often a free resource. Good positioning, destructible objects, and ranged interactions save actions, spell slots, and consumables.

> [!tip] Core habit
> Before attacking, check whether the room can solve the fight: ladders, ledges, chandeliers, barrels, chokepoints, and levers can be stronger than a normal action.

## 🧱 Destructible Objects

**Markers:** ⭐ ⚠️ ⏱️

Doors and chests can be destroyed. Chest contents remain intact. This is a practical solution when no lockpicks are available or a lock is too difficult.

### 💥 When Destruction Is Useful

| Object | Benefit |
|---|---|
| Locked door | Progress without lockpicking |
| Locked chest | Secure contents when no suitable character is available |
| Ladder | Enemies lose access or must take a longer route |
| Bridge or walkway | Enemies are split up or fall |

Not every object should be destroyed. First check whether noise, sightlines, or starting combat could become a problem.

## 🪜 Vertical Control

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️

Ladders, bridges, and walkways are often more important than individual enemies. Destroying or blocking access points forces enemies into longer routes and buys turns.

High ground is especially relevant for ranged characters and casters. Enemies that need to climb or take detours lose pressure. Your party should avoid starting difficult fights grouped together in a low or narrow position.

### 🚧 Route Denial

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️ 🎯

Route denial is useful when it prevents enemy actions rather than merely causing damage. A destroyed ladder or blocked bridge can be stronger than a small attack if it keeps several enemies from reaching the party.

| Action | Best use |
|---|---|
| Destroy a ladder | Stop melee enemies from reaching high ground |
| Break a bridge or walkway | Split enemy groups or force detours |
| Hold a chokepoint | Make enemies cluster for area effects |
| Move the party before combat | Start with ranged characters in safer positions |

If enemies are already grouped below the party, denying access can turn a dangerous fight into several controlled turns.

## 🕳️ Edges, Chasms, and Routes

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️ 🎯

**Shove** and **Throw** become much stronger when the environment supports them. Enemies near edges, on walkways, or next to hazardous surfaces are often better targets than enemies in open space.

Strength elixirs improve these plays. See [Elixirs and Potions](06_Elixirs_and_Potions.md).

## 🛢️ Common Combat Objects

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️

Chandeliers, barrels, oil, and explosives can shorten fights significantly. Before combat, quickly check for hanging objects, chokepoints, and flammable surfaces.

| Object | Common benefit |
|---|---|
| Chandelier | Damage against grouped enemies or key targets |
| Oil | Prepares a surface that can be ignited |
| Explosive barrels | High area damage |
| Chokepoints | Group and control enemies |

These objects are strongest when enemies are already grouped or are prevented from moving by initiative pressure and control.

Barrels and explosives can also be moved into groups with Throw or Telekinesis before being ignited. This is strongest when the party is already outside the blast area.

### 🧯 Spell Surfaces

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️ ✨ 🎯

Spells can reshape the battlefield even when their direct damage is not the main point.

| Surface or spell | Practical use |
|---|---|
| Web | Soft landing target when Feather Fall is unavailable; also slows and restrains enemies |
| Grease | Difficult terrain and prone risk; can be ignited or used to extend an existing fire surface |
| Ray of Frost / ice tools | Extinguish fire, create water/ice follow-up, or slow movement |
| Create or Destroy Water | Reveal likely invisible targets, extinguish flames, or set up lightning and ice follow-ups |
| Darkness / Fog Cloud | Block line of sight and create obscured space for repositioning |

Do not layer surfaces casually. Fire, ice, water, and difficult terrain can hurt the party's pathing as easily as the enemy's.

Source: [9 Hidden Spell Interactions in Baldur's Gate 3 - Toyhouze](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XOpwzSepoBk).

### 🎒 Bagged Explosives

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️ 🎯

A container can be used to move several explosive consumables as one setup package. If the container breaks open near enemies, the contents can then be ignited with fire or another suitable trigger.

Use this as an advanced setup tool, not as the default answer to every fight:

- prepare it only when the blast area is clear of allies,
- keep the party outside the chain reaction,
- check whether the fight deserves the consumable cost,
- avoid using rare consumables when normal control or positioning is enough.

Source: [13 Things That are Actually Overpowered in Baldur's Gate 3 - Toyhouze](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1g8TJBjPfqw&t=13s).

### 📦 Move Objects Without Spending Actions

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️ 🎯

Some objects can be dragged from the world or inventory and placed on the ground without spending a combat action. This is useful for setting up surfaces, blocking routes, or placing a thrown consumable where an existing hazard will trigger it.

Practical uses:

- place a small explosive or fire item into an existing damaging surface,
- drop a potion near the edge of a safe trigger area, then move through the effect deliberately,
- block a narrow route with objects when the party needs time,
- use doors and chokepoints as funnels instead of fighting in open space.

Be careful with this trick. If the surface can hurt allies, test the line and movement path before committing. Do not turn every fight into inventory fiddling; use it when it saves a real action or prevents enemy movement.

Source video: [Baldur's Gate 3: 20+ Easily Missed Tips (Spoiler Free) - Proxy Gate Tactician](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BGRu99XLSRc&t=352s).

### 🎯 Object Priority

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️

Use environmental objects when they achieve more than a normal action would.

| Question | If yes |
|---|---|
| Can one object hit several enemies? | Prefer the object over a single-target attack |
| Can it remove enemy movement? | Use it early in the fight |
| Can it start a chain reaction? | Check party positioning first |
| Can it damage allies? | Reposition or choose a safer action |

Explosives and fire surfaces are most valuable when the party has already avoided the danger zone.

For hanging objects, check whether a chain, hinge, or support can be targeted directly. Tactical view can help predict where the object will fall before spending the shot.

## 🏹 Trigger Levers at Range

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⏱️ 🎯

Some levers can be activated with ranged attacks or spells. In timed areas, activating levers from a distance can save several rounds.

This rule is generally worth remembering under time pressure: if a lever, switch, or target object is visible, check whether a ranged attack or spell can activate it before running to it.

### ⏳ Time-Limited Areas

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⏱️

In time-limited areas, movement is often the real resource. A ranged lever activation can be better than dashing, because it lets the acting character spend movement elsewhere.

Practical sequence:

1. Identify visible levers, switches, or doors.
2. Check whether line of sight exists.
3. Use a ranged attack, cantrip, spell, or thrown object if it can trigger the mechanism.
4. Spend movement on rescuing, repositioning, or reaching the next objective.

Multi-hit options such as **Magic Missile** can be especially useful when several visible levers must be opened quickly.

> [!warning]- Spoiler: named timed-area example
> The Iron Throne is the key example: triggering cell levers from range saves actions and movement at the same time.

Related spell and combat terms are summarized in [[15_Appendix#^glossary-spells|Glossary: Spells]] and [[15_Appendix#^glossary-combat|Glossary: Combat and Build Terms]].

## 📦 Stack Objects for Access

**Markers:** ⭐ ⏱️ 🎯

Crates and other movable objects can sometimes create improvised routes to higher places or awkward ledges. This is slower than jumping or flying, but useful when the party lacks movement tools.

Use this mainly outside combat. In combat, spending actions and movement on object stacking is usually too expensive unless it solves the whole encounter.

Stacked objects can also create temporary high ground for ranged attacks or thrown weapons. Treat this as a setup tool, not a default turn-by-turn habit: it is useful when a prepared position gives better line of sight, safer terrain, or a meaningful thrown-object angle.

Source videos: [I Wish I Knew These Tips Before Starting Baldur's Gate 3 - Jolsn](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lJCwezyHNlA), [Baldur's Gate 3 - Tips and Tricks For The 9 Skills Every Class in The Game Has - Proxy Gate Tactician](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Rz90AMvpM64&t=60s).

## 🔎 Look Before Combat Starts

A short environment check prevents many hard fights:

1. Are there high-ground positions for your party?
2. Are there ladders, bridges, or walkways?
3. Are enemies standing near edges?
4. Are chandeliers or other objects hanging above enemies?
5. Are there oil surfaces, barrels, or explosives?
6. Can levers or cells be activated at range?

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

**Markers:** ⚠️

| Mistake | Better habit |
|---|---|
| Starting combat with the whole party grouped low | Split and position before triggering combat |
| Ignoring ladders | Destroy or control access routes early |
| Spending actions on enemies while explosives are available | Check objects before attacking |
| Running to every lever | Test ranged activation first |
| Standing near barrels or oil | Move before using fire or explosions |

## ✅ Practical Environment Plan

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️

Before a difficult encounter, use this order:

1. Split the party if positioning matters.
2. Put ranged characters and casters where they can see key targets.
3. Identify ladders, bridges, and chokepoints.
4. Check for chandeliers, oil, barrels, and explosives.
5. Decide whether the first round is about damage, control, or route denial.
6. Start combat only after the party is outside obvious hazard zones.
