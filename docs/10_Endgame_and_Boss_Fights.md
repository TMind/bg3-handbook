## 🧰 Prepare Boss Fights

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️

Late bosses are significantly harder on higher difficulties. Preparation with elixirs, potions, condition removal, and initiative matters more than in regular fights.

> [!warning]- Spoiler: example late bosses
> Examples include Cazador and Raphael.

## ✅ Before the Fight

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️

| Preparation | Benefit |
|---|---|
| Inspect enemies | Identify resistances, immunities, and special statuses |
| Plan initiative | Stop dangerous enemies before their turns |
| Drink elixirs | Secure long-term buffs |
| Distribute potions and scrolls | Keep every character functional |
| Keep [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^remedial-potion\|Remedial Potions]] ready | Remove negative conditions faster |
| Check the environment | Use height, chokepoints, and objects |

Elixirs should be drunk directly. Potions can be thrown depending on the situation, such as throwing [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^potion-of-speed\|Potion of Speed]] before combat onto a tightly grouped party. See [Elixirs and Potions](06_Elixirs_and_Potions.md).

## 🍷 Choose Elixirs by Role

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️ 🍷

The best elixir depends on what the character must accomplish in the fight.

| Role or plan | Strong option |
|---|---|
| Caster needs the first turn | [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-vigilance\|Elixir of Vigilance]] |
| Character must survive heavy damage | [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-universal-resistance\|Elixir of Universal Resistance]] |
| Damage dealer can kill adds repeatedly | [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-bloodlust\|Elixir of Bloodlust]] |
| Strength-based attacker or thrower | [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-hill-giant-strength\|Hill Giant Strength]] or [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-cloud-giant-strength\|Cloud Giant Strength]] |
| Party wants long-term Bless value | [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-heroism\|Elixir of Heroism]] |
| Caster needs more spell slots | [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-arcane-cultivation\|Elixir of Arcane Cultivation]] |

Avoid choosing elixirs automatically. A strong general elixir can be weaker than a situational one that matches the actual encounter.

## 🧯 Plan Remedial Potions

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

In the endgame, every character should ideally carry a [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^remedial-potion\|Remedial Potion]]. It removes several negative conditions and can prevent a lost turn in boss fights.

See [Elixirs and Potions](06_Elixirs_and_Potions.md).

## ⏱️ Initiative and Control

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️

In hard fights, the first turn is often decisive. **[[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-vigilance\|Elixir of Vigilance]]** is especially strong for casters, Gloom Stalkers, and parties that want to disable or kill enemies early.

If a boss or elite enemy will not die in one round, check control options. This is especially important if the enemy acts soon.

### 🎯 First-Round Priorities

| Priority | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Stop enemies that act early | Prevents damage and control effects |
| Remove or disable adds | Makes [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-bloodlust\|Bloodlust]] and action economy stronger |
| Protect fragile key characters | Keeps casters and support characters active |
| Use terrain immediately | Route denial is strongest before enemies spread out |

The first round should have a plan. Random damage into a boss is often weaker than removing the most dangerous upcoming action.

## 🐉 Single Bosses and Adds

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️ 🎯

**[[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-bloodlust\|Elixir of Bloodlust]]** is very strong when kills are possible regularly. Against single bosses without adds, it is weaker because the effect requires kills.

In those fights, other elixirs may be better:

- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-heroism\|Heroism]] for Bless and temporary hit points,
- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-universal-resistance\|Universal Resistance]] for tanks,
- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-arcane-cultivation\|Arcane Cultivation]] for casters,
- Giant Strength for Strength-based characters.

## 💎 Consumables Are for These Fights

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

Endgame boss fights are exactly where rare consumables should be used. Saving every strong potion for later often means never gaining value from it.

Good candidates for serious fights:

- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^potion-of-speed\|Potion of Speed]] for opening tempo,
- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^remedial-potion\|Remedial Potion]] for condition-heavy encounters,
- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^potion-of-supreme-healing\|Supreme Healing]] for tanks and critical recoveries,
- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-universal-resistance\|Universal Resistance]] for characters expected to take repeated damage,
- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^potion-of-angelic-slumber\|Angelic Slumber]] before the fight if one character needs resources restored.

Use resources when they prevent lost turns, failed concentration, or deaths.

## 🤖 Late-Game Construct Encounters

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

Late-game construct enemies behave more intelligently in newer versions. Fights against them should not rely on outdated exploits or passive enemy behavior.

Before fighting them, take positioning, escape routes, and consumables seriously. Outdated assumptions about passive behavior can be especially costly here.

> [!warning]- Spoiler: named construct encounter
> Steel Watchers behave more intelligently in newer versions.
>
> Allied NPCs now try to leave Self-Destruct range instead of standing still. Steel Watchers also no longer keep chasing endlessly through the city after fights. These changes make the encounters less exploitable, but more readable.

## 🚫 Removed or Changed Tricks

**Markers:** ⭐ ⚠️

Some old exploit strategies were removed and some late-game support tools were improved. Such changes matter only when they affect a concrete tactic.

Practical rule: do not base a strategy on exploits that only work in old videos or old patch states. If a trick would be central to the fight plan, have a normal fallback plan.

> [!warning]- Spoiler: removed named exploit
> The Victoria corpse exploit was removed.
>
> The Act 3 cheese using Victoria's death-fog corpse no longer works as a way to trivialize large parts of the city.

## 🛡️ Buffed Bosses on Higher Difficulties

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

On Tactician and other harder settings, late bosses are more reliable threats. They can have correct Legendary Resistance stacks, additional abilities, and fewer simple cheese options.

> [!warning]- Spoiler: example late bosses and abilities
> Examples include Cazador, Raphael, and similar encounters. Some versions can include additional abilities such as bat swarm-style forms.

Do not assume that old fall-damage or positioning tricks will remove a major boss for free. Plan the fight around normal mechanics: initiative, control, consumables, terrain, and backup healing.

## 🧩 Patch-Relevant Habits

**Markers:** ⭐ ⚠️

Some old tactics become unreliable when enemy behavior or exploits change. The safest habit is to build plans around normal mechanics:

- inspect enemies,
- read initiative,
- prepare resources,
- control dangerous actions,
- use terrain,
- keep a backup plan if an exploit fails.

This matters most in endgame encounters because a failed assumption can cost more resources than a normal fight.

## 🏅 Honour Mode Boss Knowledge

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

Honour Mode changes many boss fights through Legendary Actions or fight-specific adjustments. A plan that works on lower difficulties may fail if it ignores those extra reactions, resistances, or encounter rules.

Before an Honour Mode boss:

1. Inspect the boss and adds.
2. Read unusual statuses before attacking.
3. Plan the first round around the boss's extra reaction or Legendary Action.
4. Spend consumables earlier than in lower difficulties.
5. Keep a backup recovery plan instead of assuming burst damage will end the fight immediately.

Source videos: [Ultimate Honour Mode Guide in Baldur's Gate 3 - NorZZa](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DPTy_gMJlhc), [All Baldur's Gate 3 Honour Mode Boss Changes - Pheeon](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cATFmsBFHHA).

## ⚠️ Difficult Individual Enemies

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

Some named enemies become much harder on Tactician or higher difficulties. Enemies with unusual status indicators or strong reactions should be inspected before attacking.

Do not treat unusual side encounters as free loot on higher difficulties. Prepare them like real fights.

> [!warning]- Spoiler: named difficult encounter
> Houndmaster Pol is much harder on Tactician.
>
> The ghost dog encounter should not be treated as free loot on higher difficulties.

## 🤝 Endgame Allies

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️

Endgame allies were improved and can matter more in late fights than before. Treat them as a real resource, not just an emergency button.

Good ally preparation can make late-game set-piece fights noticeably easier.

> [!warning]- Spoiler: named endgame allies
> Relevant improved allies include Harpers, Gur Hunters, Florrick's cohort, Nightbringer Adepts, the Armored Owlbear, Mizora, and the spell from Mizora's Boon.

## ⚔️ Githyanki Parry Indicator

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

Githyanki can explicitly show **Ready to Parry** when their parry reaction is available. Inspect the status before committing a major attack, so the strongest hit is not wasted into a ready reaction.

## ✅ Endgame Checklist

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️

| Question | If no |
|---|---|
| Does every character have healing or access to it? | Distribute potions |
| Does every character have an answer to negative conditions? | Distribute [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^remedial-potion\|Remedial Potions]] |
| Are elixirs chosen for the fight? | Weigh [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-bloodlust\|Bloodlust]], [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-heroism\|Heroism]], [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-vigilance\|Vigilance]], or resistances |
| Are caster resources prepared? | Check [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-arcane-cultivation\|Arcane Cultivation]] or [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^potion-of-angelic-slumber\|Angelic Slumber]] |
| Has the environment been read? | Check height, edges, ladders, and objects |
| Have enemies been inspected? | Do it before the first major attack |
