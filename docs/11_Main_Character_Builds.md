# 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Main Character Builds

This chapter keeps practical, spoiler-free build templates for the characters and camp supports used in this handbook. It is not a full class guide. The goal is to make party roles clear and prevent build choices from fighting the preparation routine.

> [!tip] Core habit
> Decide whether a character is **active party**, **camp support**, or **utility specialist** before choosing multiclass levels. A great active build is often a bad camp build, and the reverse is also true.

## ✅ Build Principles

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

| Principle | Practical meaning |
|---|---|
| Active characters need combat value | Do not delay key combat levels just to gain minor camp utility |
| Camp characters need coverage, not damage | Buff access matters more than perfect combat stats |
| One character should not do everything | Class features like Transmuter Stone and Weapon Bond need separate specialists |
| Concentration must be planned | Do not stack several concentration plans onto the same active caster |
| Daily buffs should not drain the active party | Use camp supports for Longstrider, extra Death Ward, Song of Rest, and utility setup |

## ⚔️ Lae'zel: Battle Master Frontline Pin ^laezel-build

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️ 🎯

Keep Lae'zel as a **Battle Master Fighter** unless you deliberately want a different martial identity. This fits the party better than a generic damage build because the party wins by locking enemies inside Web, prone, and Spirit Guardians.

| Level plan | Role | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Battle Master Fighter 12 | Frontline tank, GWM damage, control maneuvers | Keeps Fighter feats, Action Surge, Improved Extra Attack, and strong maneuver scaling |

### Practical Setup

| Choice | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Fighting style | Great Weapon Fighting or Defence |
| Key feats | Great Weapon Master, Strength ASI to 20, Sentinel, Savage Attacker / Alert |
| Core maneuvers | Trip Attack, Pushing Attack, Disarming Attack |
| Later maneuvers | Precision Attack for GWM accuracy, Menacing Attack or Riposte by taste |
| Buffs | Longstrider, Aid, Warding Bond if TMind can absorb the shared damage, Potion of Speed for serious fights |

### Feat Priority

| Timing | Pick | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Already taken | Great Weapon Master | Main damage engine |
| Next if Strength is not 20 | +1 Strength / +1 Constitution or another route to Strength 20 | GWM needs accuracy before more control feats |
| After Strength 20 | Sentinel | Locks enemies inside Web, Spirit Guardians, and chokepoints |
| Later | Savage Attacker / Alert | More damage consistency or better opening tempo |

### Party Role

Lae'zel should engage first or immediately after Kao's control lands. Her job is not only damage; it is to make enemies spend turns badly.

1. Jump or move to the enemy cluster.
2. Use Trip Attack on priority melee targets.
3. Use Pushing Attack to push enemies back into Web, Spirit Guardians, or hazards.
4. Use Sentinel once available to stop enemies from leaving the kill zone.
5. Use Action Surge to remove priority targets, not to overkill trash.

Battle Master is source-supported as a tactical Fighter subclass because its maneuvers disable, hinder, or support while spending Superiority Dice that recover on rests.

## 🗡️ Astarion: Thief Utility or Gloom Stalker Upgrade ^astarion-build

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⏱️ 🎯

Astarion currently works as a **Thief Rogue**: stealth opener, Sneak Attack, locks, traps, and utility. That is valid and simple. The stronger online-optimized path is a stealth burst multiclass, but it asks the player to start fights from stealth more consistently.

| Build | Role | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Thief Rogue 12 | Simple scout, lockpicker, steady Sneak Attack, extra bonus action | Keep this if you value simplicity and utility |
| Rogue 4 / Gloom Stalker Ranger 5 / Champion Fighter 3 | Strong stealth opener, Extra Attack, Action Surge, high first-round burst | Use this if you want a more optimized assassin-style Astarion |
| Rogue 4 / Gloom Stalker Ranger 5 / Fighter 3 with Thief instead of Assassin | More bonus-action flexibility, less surprise-round dependence | Best compromise if you do not always force surprise starts |

### Practical Setup

| Choice | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Current core | Keep Thief until you are ready to respec around Ranger 5 Extra Attack |
| Must-have skills | Sleight of Hand, Stealth, Perception if available |
| Combat habit | Trigger Sneak Attack through stealth, advantage, prone targets, or an ally adjacent to the target |
| Party synergy | Lae'zel Trip Attack and Kao control create Sneak Attack targets |
| Strong spell support | Greater Invisibility is excellent because it can maintain advantage across turns |
| Trap to avoid | Magical Darkness does not help unless Astarion can see through it |

### Careful Review Note

The common optimized assassin guide path is **Assassin Rogue 4 / Gloom Stalker Ranger 5 / Champion Fighter 3**. It is powerful because Gloom Stalker improves the opening round and Fighter adds Action Surge. For this party, do not switch blindly: if fights usually start after dialogue or without surprise, **Thief** may be more comfortable than Assassin.

## 🧙 Kao: Human Mage / Conjuration Control Wizard ^kao-build

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ✨ 🎯

Keep Kao as a control-first Wizard. The party already has martial damage and Spirit Guardians pressure; Kao's job is to decide where enemies are allowed to stand.

| Level plan | Role | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Conjuration Wizard 12 | Battlefield control, utility, Counterspell, scroll learning | Keeps full Wizard progression and supports Web/Grease positioning plans |
| Evocation Wizard 12 | Safer blasting near allies | Use only if you want Gale-style safe Fireball play more than control identity |
| Divination Wizard 12 | Roll control and reliability | Strong alternative if control saves are the main problem |

### Practical Setup

| Need | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Main stats | Intelligence first, then Constitution and Dexterity |
| Feats | Intelligence ASI, Alert, War Caster / Resilient: Constitution if concentration breaks too often |
| Core concentration | Web, Hold Person, Slow, or another control spell; do not compete with yourself |
| Must-prepare utility | Counterspell, Misty Step, Longstrider if nobody else has it, Knock, Feather Fall, See Invisibility when relevant |
| Combat rule | Place control on enemies, not between allies and enemies |
| Reaction setup | Counterspell must be prepared and its reaction set to Ask or On |

### Party Role

Kao makes the aura blender work. Web, Grease, Hold Person, and chokepoint control keep enemies where TMind and Lae'zel want them. Fireball is secondary: use it when enemies are grouped and allies are not standing in the blast plan.

Wizard guide sources agree on the core principle that Wizard is strongest through spell breadth, Intelligence scaling, and careful spell-slot management; the subclass does not prevent learning other Wizard spells.

## 💀 TMind: Drow Death Cleric / Necromancer Aura ^tmind-build

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️ ✨

TMind is not a backline healer. Treat him as an off-tank aura caster and necromantic pressure piece: he enters after the pin starts, carries Spirit Guardians, and uses Healing Word only when needed.

| Build | Role | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Death Domain Cleric 12 | Simple aura cleric, full Cleric spell access, final feat | Best if TMind must provide Heroes' Feast and full Cleric utility |
| Death Cleric 10 / Circle of Stars Druid 2 | Spirit Guardians specialist with stronger concentration support and Starry Form utility | Strong if another Cleric handles Heroes' Feast and late Cleric buffs |

### Practical Setup

| Choice | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Main stats | Wisdom first, then Constitution and Dexterity |
| Feats | Wisdom ASI, War Caster or Resilient: Constitution, Alert / Tough by need |
| Core concentration | Spirit Guardians for meaningful fights |
| Daily support | Warding Bond on Lae'zel only if TMind's HP and healing plan can support shared damage |
| Necromancer identity | Use Death Domain offense and summons as support, but do not let summons break party positioning |
| Item synergy | Healing Word can trigger on-heal gear; this can add Bless-style value without using concentration |
| Divine Intervention | Keep TMind's own use as an emergency button; use Shadowheart for the weapon trick instead |

### Necromancer Logistics

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ 🎯

Necromancer turns become smoother when the needed bodies are prepared before the fight. If the build depends on raising corpses, keep a small supply available instead of hoping the next area provides ideal targets.

Practical habits:

- after a fight, pick up suitable corpses when carrying capacity allows it,
- send extras to camp instead of leaving the build without fuel,
- remove corpses that enemy casters might use against the party,
- do not clutter the active inventory; this is a camp-storage tool, not normal loot handling.

Source video: [Baldur's Gate 3: 20+ Easily Missed Tips (Spoiler Free) - Proxy Gate Tactician](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BGRu99XLSRc&t=501s).

### Party Role

1. Let Lae'zel or control magic start the pin.
2. Move TMind into the cluster with Spirit Guardians active.
3. Use Healing Word as a bonus-action rescue, not as a full healing plan.
4. Keep Warding Bond damage in mind; if Lae'zel takes repeated hits, TMind is also paying for them.
5. Short Rest after serious aura fights to restore HP even when spell slots remain spent.

The online Death Cleric / Stars Druid build is attractive for Spirit Guardians because Starry Form can support concentration and bonus-action value. The tradeoff is real: going Cleric 10 / Druid 2 means losing Cleric 11 spell access such as Heroes' Feast on TMind.

## 🏕️ Bench and Camp Options ^camp-options

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⏱️ ✨

These characters are not part of the default four-person plan, but the local notes show clear swap cases.

| Character | Use when | What changes |
|---|---|---|
| Gale | The fight rewards AoE damage more than stealth, traps, or precision execution | Swap for Astarion; gain safe blasting if Evocation, lose lockpicking/scout pressure |
| Shadowheart | The party wants extra radiant/fire support, Bless coverage, or a second Cleric toolkit | Do not treat her as a Lae'zel replacement unless the frontline plan changes |

### Shadowheart Support Note

Shadowheart is useful as a backline Light Cleric support option: Bless, Spiritual Weapon, Healing Word, Radiance of the Dawn, Fireball, Silence, and Aid all fit the broader party plan. The important constraint is concentration: Shadowheart, TMind, and Kao should not overwrite each other's key concentration effects.

### Shadowheart Divine Intervention Trick

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ 💰 ✨ 🎯

Once Shadowheart reaches Cleric level 10, she can use **Divine Intervention: Arm Thy Servant** in camp, create **Devotee's Mace**, and hand the mace to TMind. This spends Shadowheart's once-per-character Divine Intervention, not TMind's.

| Step | Practical result |
|---|---|
| Bring Shadowheart into the active party | She needs access to her Cleric level 10 action |
| Use Divine Intervention: Arm Thy Servant | Creates Devotee's Mace in Shadowheart's inventory |
| Move Devotee's Mace to TMind | TMind gains the weapon without spending his own Divine Intervention |
| Keep TMind's Divine Intervention unused | Save it for Opulent Revival as a true emergency button |

Use **Blood of Lathander** as TMind's default weapon unless a fight specifically wants Devotee's Mace healing aura or extra on-hit Radiant damage. Blood of Lathander is usually the safer everyday cleric weapon; Devotee's Mace is a strong swap option.

Source: [bg3.wiki: Divine Intervention](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Divine_Intervention), [bg3.wiki: Devotee's Mace](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Devotee%27s_Mace).

## 🎵 Hireling Bard: Camp Support ^hireling-bard

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⏱️ ✨

Use the Bard hireling to extend the adventuring day and cover non-Cleric support without weakening active Shadowheart.

| Level plan | Role | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| College of Lore Bard 10-12 | Longstrider, Song of Rest, Freedom of Movement, Magical Secrets utility | Clean support path with no complicated multiclassing |

### Level Priorities

| Level | Pick | Practical value |
|---|---|---|
| Bard 1 | Longstrider | Daily movement ritual |
| Bard 3 | College of Lore | Support subclass |
| Bard 7 | Freedom of Movement | Strong long-duration movement/control protection |
| Bard 10 | Magical Secrets: Death Ward | Extra daily insurance if Shadowheart has other priorities |
| Bard 10+ | Second Magical Secrets pick | Choose utility or party need |

Song of Rest is one of the best reasons to keep a Bard hireling. It effectively adds another Short Rest-style recovery to the day.

## 🧪 Camp Specialists ^camp-specialists

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ 💰 ⏱️ 🎯

These are not active-party builds. They exist to save resources, prepare the party, or solve a narrow problem.

| Specialist | Build | Practical use |
|---|---|---|
| Full camp buffer | Cleric 11 / Wizard 1 | Aid, Warding Bond, Death Ward, Freedom of Movement, Heroes' Feast, Longstrider, Mage Armor |
| Goodberry buffer | Cleric 11 / Druid 1 | Cleric buff core plus Goodberry |
| Alchemy crafter | Transmutation Wizard 6+ | Experimental Alchemy and Transmuter Stone |
| Weapon binder | Eldritch Knight Fighter 3+ | Weapon Bond for valuable or thrown weapons |

Camp specialists should not replace active builds unless the party actually needs them in combat.

## ⚔️ Frontliner Template

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️

Use this for a martial companion whose job is to stand in danger, control space, and convert buffs into damage.

| Template | Role | Works well with |
|---|---|---|
| Fighter 12 | Reliable attacks, Action Surge, feats | Longstrider, Hill/Cloud Giant Strength, Potion of Speed |
| Barbarian 8 / Fighter 4 | Durable burst attacker | Giant Strength, Bloodlust, thrown or melee plans |
| Paladin 12 | Aura support and burst damage | Strength elixirs, Aid, Death Ward |

Keep this slot simple if the rest of the party is already resource-heavy.

## 🗡️ Scout and Skill Template

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⏱️ 🎯

Use this for lockpicking, scouting, traps, stealth starts, and high-value skill checks.

| Template | Role | Works well with |
|---|---|---|
| Rogue 12 | Simple skill specialist and sneak attacker | Invisibility, Enhance Ability, special arrows |
| Rogue 4 / Ranger 8 | Scout with stronger combat profile | Longstrider, high Dexterity, initiative bonuses |
| Bard 10+ | Face, skills, support magic | Enhance Ability, Bardic Inspiration, Song of Rest |

The skill character should carry utility scrolls, invisibility tools, and trap-handling supplies.

## 🔁 Build Review Checklist ^build-review

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⏱️

Use this before respeccing:

1. Is this character active or camp-only?
2. Which concentration spell is their default combat plan?
3. Which Long-Rest buffs do they provide?
4. Are we delaying an important class level?
5. Does another character already cover the same job?
6. Are the key consumables available for this build?
7. Does the build still work without rare late-game items?
