# 🧰 Preparation and Battle Setup

> [!tip] Core habit
> Separate daily setup from fight setup. Long-Rest buffs should be handled once after resting; short buffs and consumables should be chosen for the next specific fight.

## ✅ Daily Setup After Long Rest ^daily-setup

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⏱️ ⚔️

These are the first checks after a Long Rest. The goal is to avoid remembering them right before a difficult fight.

| Setup | Why it matters | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Drink planned elixirs | Secures Until Long Rest buffs | One elixir slot per character |
| Cast Longstrider | More movement all day | No concentration; excellent default buff |
| Cast Mage Armor on eligible casters | Raises AC for unarmored characters | Only useful if the character is not wearing armor |
| Cast Aid | More maximum HP for the party | Strong before difficult areas |
| Prepare key spells | Avoid entering fights with the wrong spell loadout | Especially important for casters |
| Refill consumables | Makes potions, scrolls, and bombs reachable | See [Inventory and Equipment](04_Inventory_and_Equipment.md) |
| Visit key vendors | Rebuild potion and ingredient stock | See [Vendors and Resources](07_Vendors_and_Resources.md) |

## 🍷 Elixirs Before Adventuring ^daily-elixirs

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️ 🍷

Elixirs should normally be drunk directly after the Long Rest or before entering a dangerous area. Do not throw them unless a specific short-duration effect is intended.

| Role or plan | Strong elixir | Practical use |
|---|---|---|
| First-turn caster or alpha-strike character | [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-vigilance\|Elixir of Vigilance]] | Acts early and avoids surprise |
| Strength attacker, thrower, or carrier | [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-hill-giant-strength\|Hill Giant Strength]] / [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-cloud-giant-strength\|Cloud Giant Strength]] | Damage, carrying capacity, Shove, Throw |
| Multi-kill damage dealer | [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-bloodlust\|Elixir of Bloodlust]] | Extra actions after kills |
| Character expected to take heavy damage | [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-universal-resistance\|Universal Resistance]] | Major defensive value |
| Party wants Bless without concentration | [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-heroism\|Heroism]] | Long-term Bless and temporary HP |
| Caster needs more spell slots | [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-arcane-cultivation\|Arcane Cultivation]] | More resources before hard fights |

> [!warning] Common mistake
> Throwing important elixirs can shorten or break the intended Until Long Rest value. Drink key elixirs directly.

## ✨ Useful Long-Rest Buffs ^long-rest-buffs

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️ ✨

This is not a complete spell list. These are practical buffs worth remembering because they can be applied before combat and provide lasting value.

Short explanations of these buffs are in [[15_Appendix#^glossary-spells|Glossary: Spells, Buffs, and Magical Effects]].

| Buff | Duration style | Concentration | Best use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Longstrider | Until Long Rest | No | Default mobility buff |
| Mage Armor | Until Long Rest | No | Unarmored casters |
| Aid | Until Long Rest | No | More party durability |
| Protection from Poison | Long duration | No | Poison-heavy areas or enemies |
| Warding Bond | Until Long Rest | No | Advanced defensive setup; splits damage with the caster |
| Death Ward | Until Long Rest | No | Protects key characters from a lethal drop |
| Freedom of Movement | Until Long Rest | No | Movement/control-heavy encounters |
| Heroes' Feast | Until Long Rest | No | Late-game durability and protection |

## 🧙 Who Can Cast the Core Buffs ^buff-caster-access

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⏱️ ✨

Use this table when building camp casters or deciding who must prepare which spell. Levels refer to class level in BG3, not total character level after multiclassing.

| Buff or setup | Practical caster access | Earliest practical access | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Longstrider | Bard, Druid, Ranger, Wizard; also Eldritch Knight / Arcane Trickster access | Level 1 for Bard/Druid/Wizard; Ranger level 2 | Ritual Caster feat can also cover it; excellent camp-caster spell |
| Mage Armor | Sorcerer, Wizard; also Eldritch Knight / Arcane Trickster access | Level 1 | Only matters for unarmored targets and some summons |
| Aid | Cleric, Paladin | Cleric level 3; Paladin level 5 | Upcast when spell slots allow it |
| Protection from Poison | Cleric, Druid, Ranger, Paladin | Level 3 for Cleric/Druid; Ranger/Paladin level 5 | Keep the spell prepared if removing it would drop the buff |
| Warding Bond | Cleric | Level 3 | Strong advanced camp-casting option; watch caster damage |
| Death Ward | Cleric; Bard through Magical Secrets | Cleric level 7; Bard level 10 via Magical Secrets | Use on key characters before high-risk sections |
| Freedom of Movement | Bard, Cleric, Druid | Level 7 | Best before terrain, restraint, or paralysis-heavy sections |
| Heroes' Feast | Cleric, Druid | Level 11 | Late-game daily setup; stacks well with Aid |
| Goodberry | Druid, Ranger | Druid level 1; Ranger level 2 | Useful as cheap healing and for on-heal item triggers |
| Transmuter Stone | Transmutation Wizard | Wizard level 6 | Movement stone is the simple default |
| Weapon Bond | Eldritch Knight Fighter | Fighter level 3 with Eldritch Knight | Useful against disarm and for thrown-weapon setups |

If a buff is central to the plan, assign it to a specific character instead of relying on memory. This is especially important for camp casters, because prepared-spell changes can remove some active buffs.

## 🧙 Recommended Camp Buffer Builds ^camp-buffer-builds

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ✨ 🎯

A pure Wizard is not the best single character for daily buffs. The strongest core is usually **Cleric**, because Cleric covers Aid, Protection from Poison, Warding Bond, Death Ward, Freedom of Movement, and Heroes' Feast.

| Build | What it covers | What it misses | When to use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cleric 11 / Wizard 1 | Aid, Protection from Poison, Warding Bond, Death Ward, Freedom of Movement, Heroes' Feast, Longstrider, Mage Armor | Goodberry, Transmuter Stone, Weapon Bond | Best single-character camp buffer |
| Cleric 11 / Druid 1 | Aid, Protection from Poison, Warding Bond, Death Ward, Freedom of Movement, Heroes' Feast, Longstrider, Goodberry | Mage Armor, Transmuter Stone, Weapon Bond | Best if Goodberry matters more than Mage Armor |
| Cleric 12 | All major Cleric buffs with one more Cleric feat | Longstrider, Mage Armor, Goodberry, Transmuter Stone, Weapon Bond | Simple and reliable; pair with a separate Wizard/Druid |
| Lore Bard 10+ | Longstrider, Freedom of Movement, Death Ward through Magical Secrets | Aid, Warding Bond, Protection from Poison, Heroes' Feast, Mage Armor | Bard-based support hireling, not the best full camp buffer |
| Bard 8 / Cleric 3 / Wizard 1 | Longstrider, Freedom of Movement, Aid, Protection from Poison, Warding Bond, Mage Armor | Death Ward, Heroes' Feast, Goodberry, Transmuter Stone, Weapon Bond | Most practical Bard-based multiclass coverage |
| Transmutation Wizard 6+ | Transmuter Stone, Longstrider, Mage Armor, alchemy support | Major Cleric buffs | Separate utility/alchemy camp caster |
| Eldritch Knight Fighter 3+ | Weapon Bond | Most spell buffs | Separate anti-disarm / thrown-weapon setup |

For the best one-character setup, use **Cleric 11 / Wizard 1**. Prioritize Wisdom for Cleric preparation and keep enough Intelligence to prepare the Wizard utility spells you actually need, usually Longstrider and Mage Armor. The Wizard spells do not need high Intelligence if they are non-offensive buffs.

Do not try to make one character do everything. Transmuter Stone and Weapon Bond are class features, not normal spells, so they usually belong on separate camp specialists.

### 🎵 Bard Hireling Route

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ✨

If the hireling should stay mostly Bard, use **College of Lore**. Lore is the clean support choice because it gets extra Magical Secrets at Bard level 6, while every Bard gets the main Magical Secrets feature at Bard level 10.

| Character level | Take | Result |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Bard | Learn Longstrider immediately |
| 3 | College of Lore | Support subclass; extra skills and Cutting Words if ever used actively |
| 6 | Additional Magical Secrets | Optional utility picks; this does not cover the missing Cleric core buffs |
| 7 | Bard level 7 | Learn Freedom of Movement |
| 10 | Magical Secrets | Take Death Ward if this Bard should cover it |
| 11-12 | Bard levels or multiclass | More Bard comfort, or switch to Cleric/Wizard if missing camp buffs matter |

A pure Bard hireling is good for **Longstrider**, **Freedom of Movement**, and **Death Ward** at Bard level 10. It is not enough for the full camp-buff package because Bard does not normally cover **Aid**, **Warding Bond**, **Protection from Poison**, or **Heroes' Feast**.

For maximum Bard-based coverage, use **Bard 8 / Cleric 3 / Wizard 1**:

| Class split | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Bard 8 | Longstrider and Freedom of Movement |
| Cleric 3 | Aid, Protection from Poison, and Warding Bond |
| Wizard 1 | Mage Armor and extra utility |

This build still misses **Death Ward** and **Heroes' Feast**, so it works best if another Cleric handles those.

## 🏕️ Daily Buff Stack Routine ^buff-stack-routine

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ✨ 🎯

Use this as an optional high-optimization routine after a Long Rest. It is strongest before difficult areas, Honour Mode attempts, or any section where failed preparation would cost many resources.

| Step | Practical value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cast Longstrider on everyone | More movement for the whole adventuring day | Ritual access makes this almost free |
| Cast Aid at the highest practical level | More maximum HP for the party and summons | Stack with other maximum-HP buffs later |
| Add Protection from Poison when relevant | Reduces poison risk before poison-heavy areas | Keep the spell prepared if the buff depends on it |
| Use Death Ward on key characters | Prevents one lethal drop | Good insurance for fragile or essential characters |
| Use Freedom of Movement before control-heavy sections | Ignores many movement problems | Best before terrain or restraint-heavy encounters |
| Add Heroes' Feast when available | Late-game durability and protection | Stacks well with Aid |
| Consider Warding Bond only for hard content | Strong damage reduction | Advanced camp-casting setup; can make fights much easier |
| Give Transmuter Stones to key characters | Extra movement or a save-focused utility stone | Movement is often the default pick |
| Cast Mage Armor on eligible summons or unarmored allies | Raises AC if no armor is worn | Skip armored targets |

Do not force the full routine for every small fight. The value is having a checklist for serious days, not turning every Long Rest into admin work.

Source: [Baldur's Gate 3: The Ultimate Buff Stacking Guide - SwoleBenji](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fjS7vvau0o0&t=21s).

## 🏕️ Camp Casting

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ✨ 🎯

Some Long-Rest buffs can be cast by characters who are not part of the active adventuring party. This can preserve active-party spell slots while still giving the party more durability or utility.

Good candidates include **Aid**, **Death Ward**, **Freedom of Movement**, **Protection from Poison**, **Warding Bond**, and similar long-duration buffs. Be careful with buffs that depend on the caster staying in the party, staying alive, keeping the spell prepared, or maintaining concentration.

Use camp casting only if it matches how much optimization you want in the run. It is powerful, but can make preparation feel mechanical if used for every minor fight.

Source videos: [Baldur's Gate 3 Camp Casting Buff Spell Guide - Jay Dunna](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gOYZjchKNJA), [Baldur's Gate 3: The Ultimate Buff Stacking Guide - SwoleBenji](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fjS7vvau0o0).

### 🧑‍🏫 Temporary Hireling Utility

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⏱️ ✨ 🎯

Hirelings are useful even when they are not permanent party members. Bring one in for a specific job, spend their restored resources, then return to the normal party.

Good temporary jobs:

- cast non-concentration daily buffs such as **Longstrider** or **Aid**,
- prepare utility spells such as **Knock** or **Feather Fall** for a specific route,
- provide extra healing between fights,
- cast light or visibility support before a dark section,
- use **Song of Rest** if the Bard hireling is part of the support plan.

Do not let this replace the basic routine. The value is solving a specific problem without draining the active party.

Source video: [Baldur's Gate 3: 20+ Easily Missed Tips (Spoiler Free) - Proxy Gate Tactician](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BGRu99XLSRc&t=98s).

## 🍓 Goodberry and On-Heal Weapon Buffs

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️ 🎯

Goodberry is not just emergency healing. If a character wears gear that adds weapon damage after being healed, eating a Goodberry before combat can trigger that effect without spending a larger healing resource.

Use this only when the party already has a convenient Goodberry source. A dedicated camp crafter can produce berries after each Long Rest, but this is an optimization rather than a required habit.

Source: [Baldur's Gate 3: The Ultimate Buff Stacking Guide - SwoleBenji](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fjS7vvau0o0&t=108s).

## 🧪 Transmuter Stones

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⏱️ 🎯

A Transmutation Wizard can create a Transmuter Stone and pass it to another character. The movement-speed stone is often the easiest default value because movement improves positioning, jumping routes, melee uptime, and retreat options.

| Stone plan | Use when | Avoid when |
|---|---|---|
| Movement speed | A character benefits from better positioning all day | Another source already solves movement |
| Constitution saving throws | A caster must protect concentration | The character rarely concentrates |
| Resistance stones | A specific damage type is expected | Warding Bond or another resistance source already covers it |

One caster normally supports one active stone. If you want several stones, prepare several Transmutation Wizards or hirelings. Treat this as advanced setup, not baseline play.

Source: [Baldur's Gate 3: The Ultimate Buff Stacking Guide - SwoleBenji](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fjS7vvau0o0&t=933s).

## ⚔️ Pre-Fight Setup ^pre-fight-setup

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️ 🎯

Use this when a fight is likely but not yet started.

| Step | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Inspect visible enemies | Reveals resistances, immunities, and reactions |
| Split the party | Prevents bad grouped positioning |
| Move ranged characters and casters | Secures line of sight and safer starts |
| Check ladders, ledges, barrels, and levers | Finds free damage or route denial |
| Distribute consumables | Makes healing, scrolls, bombs, and arrows available |
| Decide the first target | Turns initiative into a plan |
| Drink short prep consumables if needed | Avoids spending the first turn setting up |

## 🧠 Concentration Planning ^concentration-planning

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

Only one concentration spell can be maintained by the same caster at a time. Pre-fight planning should avoid overwriting an important active spell with a weaker one.

| Question | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Who is concentrating already? | Prevents accidentally canceling a stronger buff |
| Which spell must stay active during round one? | Protects the fight plan |
| Is the caster likely to be hit? | Concentration can break under pressure |
| Can an elixir or item replace the spell? | Saves concentration for something stronger |

Examples:

- **[[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-heroism\|Elixir of Heroism]]** can provide Bless-style value without using normal Bless concentration.
- A caster with **[[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-vigilance\|Elixir of Vigilance]]** is better positioned to land early control before enemies act.
- If a caster must concentrate, keep them out of obvious danger before the fight starts.

## 🎯 Short Buffs and Consumables

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️ 🎯

Short-duration buffs should be used when the fight is imminent. If the party still needs to walk, talk, or reposition for a long time, wait.

| Tool | Best timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^potion-of-speed\|Potion of Speed]] | Immediately before a hard fight | Can be thrown to buff grouped allies |
| Candle + Dip | Before or early in combat | Adds efficient fire damage, especially with multiple attacks |
| Scrolls | When the needed spell is not prepared | Keep emergency options spread across the party |
| Special arrows | Before committing ranged actions | Best on characters with good hit chance and line of sight |
| Bombs | When enemies are grouped | Check party positioning first |

## 🎲 Skill-Check Setup

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⏱️

Some encounters are easier if the right character starts the interaction.

| Setup | Use |
|---|---|
| Switch party members | Bring the right proficiency or stat |
| Enhance Ability | Improve important checks |
| Bardic Inspiration | Add reliability to key rolls |
| Strength elixir | Helps Strength checks, Shove, Throw, and carrying |
| Split the party | Ensures the correct character triggers the interaction |

Do this before clicking suspicious objects, starting important dialogue, or triggering a known hard check.

## ✅ Battle Setup Checklist ^battle-setup-checklist

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️ ⏱️

Use this checklist before hard fights:

1. Long-Rest buffs active?
2. Elixirs chosen and drunk?
3. Concentration plan clear?
4. Party split and positioned?
5. Enemies inspected?
6. First target chosen from initiative?
7. Potions, scrolls, bombs, and arrows distributed?
8. Environment checked for height, ladders, barrels, and levers?
9. Short buffs saved until the fight is truly imminent?
