## ⚠️ Core Rule

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ 💰

Plants and monster ingredients do not reliably respawn. Long-term supply of alchemy ingredients, potions, and consumables mainly comes from vendors.

Important vendors should be checked again after a Long Rest.

## 🧾 What to Buy Regularly

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ 💰

| Category | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Alchemy ingredients | Foundation for strong elixirs and potions |
| Healing potions | Saves spell slots and rescues actions |
| Special potions | Preparation for hard fights |
| Scrolls | Flexible solutions without prepared spells |
| Bombs and arrows | Immediate combat options for suitable characters |

Not every item is automatically important. Prioritize resources that clearly make fights easier or replace rare ingredients.

## 🔁 Long-Rest Loop

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ 💰 ⏱️

Vendor inventories should be checked again after Long Rests. This is especially important when specific ingredients for [Alchemy](05_Alchemy.md) or key consumables are missing.

Practical sequence:

1. Remember important vendors before a Long Rest.
2. Check their inventory after the Long Rest.
3. Buy rare ingredients and strong consumables.
4. Sort inventory by healing, elixirs, and scrolls.
5. Distribute consumables before hard fights.

## 🧭 Mark Important Vendors

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⏱️ 💰

Use map markers for vendors that matter to the current act. This saves time after Long Rests and reduces the chance of forgetting a useful restock route.

Good marker candidates:

- alchemy vendors,
- scroll vendors,
- traders with strong arrows, bombs, or potions,
- vendors near fast-travel points.

Source video: [I Wish I Knew These Tips Before Starting Baldur's Gate 3 - Jolsn](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lJCwezyHNlA).

## 📈 Level-Up Restocks

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ 💰 🎯

Vendor inventories can also refresh around level-up behavior. If a level-up is pending and a vendor has resources you care about, check the vendor before and after leveling.

This can become exploit-like if combined with repeated respecs or hirelings. Use it as much or as little as fits the run, but know the mechanic exists when planning scarce ingredients.

Source videos: [Baldur's Gate 3: 2 Methods To Restock Vendors - KpShamino](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=72PyNJbQ7j4), [Baldur's Gate 3: Vendor Inventory Reset Exploit - BigA_Gam3z](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-7xBd187U3A).

## 🌱 Act 1

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ 💰 🧪

| Vendor | Use |
|---|---|
| Arron in the Druid Grove | Early supply of basic resources |
| Roah Moonglow in the Goblin Camp | Useful goods for the early game |

### ✅ Priorities in Act 1

- Refill healing potions.
- Keep ingredients for [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-fire-resistance\|Fire Resistance]], [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-heroism\|Heroism]], and [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-vigilance\|Vigilance]].
- Do not hoard early consumables too long if they make hard fights easier.

## 🌘 Act 2

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ 💰 🧪

| Vendor | Use |
|---|---|
| Quartermaster Talli | Central supply source in Last Light Inn |
| Mattis | Additional goods around Last Light |
| Dammon | Important vendor in Last Light Inn; can also help buy back ingredients sold earlier |

Last Light Inn works well as a recurring vendor loop after Long Rests.

### ✅ Priorities in Act 2

- Secure ingredients for [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^potion-of-speed\|Potion of Speed]].
- Do not sell Warg Fangs needed for [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-bloodlust\|Bloodlust]].
- Plan [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-hill-giant-strength\|Hill Giant Strength]] for Strength builds.
- Keep [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-heroism\|Heroism]] in mind as an efficient long-term buff.

## 🏙️ Act 3

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ 💰 🧪

| Vendor | Use |
|---|---|
| Helsik in Devil's Fee | Important late-game resources |
| Bonecloak's Apothecary | Alchemy and consumable resources |

Both should be revisited after Long Rests.

### ✅ Priorities in Act 3

- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-cloud-giant-strength\|Cloud Giant Strength]] for Strength builds.
- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-universal-resistance\|Universal Resistance]] for difficult boss fights.
- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-arcane-cultivation\|Arcane Cultivation]] for casters.
- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^potion-of-supreme-healing\|Supreme Healing]] and [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^potion-of-angelic-slumber\|Angelic Slumber]] for long or resource-intensive sections.
- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^remedial-potion\|Remedial Potions]] for endgame fights.

After a few Act 3 vendor loops, the party can build a large stock of top-tier ingredients such as Divine Miasma, Zor Scales, Planetar Feathers, and other late-game resources.

## ⚠️ Do Not Sell Ingredients Too Quickly

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ 💰 🧪

Many ingredients look like clutter, but are the foundation for strong recipes. Before selling, check whether they appear in the important ingredient lists.

Be especially careful with:

- Hyena Ears,
- Warg Fangs,
- Pegasus Feather,
- Bloodstained Hook,
- Hill Giant Fingernails,
- Cloud Giant Fingernails,
- Divine Miasma,
- Planetar Feather,
- Kirin Hair.

## 🧪 Connection to Alchemy

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ 🧪

## 🎒 Connection to Inventory

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️

Purchased resources only help if they can be reached in combat. Before difficult fights, distribute healing potions, bombs, scrolls, and condition removal across several characters. See [Inventory and Equipment](04_Inventory_and_Equipment.md).
