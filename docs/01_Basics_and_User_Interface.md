# 🧭 Basics and User Interface

This chapter collects interface habits and decision routines that prevent many avoidable mistakes. The biggest advantages often come from checking the right information before taking risky actions.

> [!tip] Core habit
> Inspect first, act second. Most avoidable mistakes come from ignoring enemy statuses, initiative order, vision cones, or the current party setup.

## 🔎 Inspect Enemies

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

Enemies should be inspected regularly with right-click and **Examine**, or with `T`. Resistances, immunities, active statuses, and special reactions often determine whether an attack is worth using.

This is especially important against enemies with visible status indicators such as **Ready to Parry**. These clues can make attacks much weaker or force a different target priority.

### 🔎 When to Inspect

| Situation | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| New enemy type | Resistances and immunities prevent wasted attacks |
| Boss or elite enemy | Special reactions and statuses can change the fight plan |
| Unexpectedly low damage | A resistance or active protection effect is often the reason |
| Visible special status | Effects such as Ready to Parry should be understood before attacking |

## ⏱️ Read the Initiative Bar

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️ ⏱️

Enemies can be selected through their portraits in the initiative bar. This saves time when characters are hidden behind objects, stacked together, or hard to click in a crowded fight.

Enemies that act before your party are often the highest priority. If a dangerous enemy will not die in one round, crowd control is often better than more damage.

### 🎯 Select Targets Through Portraits

The initiative portraits are often more reliable than clicking enemy models in the combat area. This helps in crowded rooms, on uneven terrain, with large enemies, and when multiple units overlap.

Practical sequence:

1. Read the initiative bar.
2. Identify enemies that act before your next turn.
3. Select the target through its portrait.
4. Inspect it if statuses or resistances are unclear.
5. Only then attack, control, or reposition.

## 👁️ Show Vision Cones

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⏱️

`Shift` shows NPC vision cones. This matters for sneaking, pickpocketing, pre-fight positioning, and avoiding patrolling enemies.

Before risky actions, briefly press `Shift`. This helps prevent accidental detection while repositioning the party, opening containers, or setting up an encounter.

For theft, lockpicking, and pickpocketing, combine vision cones with Turn-Based Mode. This lets you time movement and interactions precisely instead of guessing around patrol routes.

## 🔗 Split the Party

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️ ⏱️

`G` splits or regroups the party. This prevents everyone from walking into traps, vision cones, or bad combat positions at the same time.

Splitting the party is especially useful before ambushes, jump sections, and situations where only one character should trigger a risky skill check.

### 🧰 Common Uses

| Use | Benefit |
|---|---|
| Positioning before combat | Ranged characters, casters, and melee characters start where they are needed |
| Checking traps or chokepoints | Only one character risks damage or detection |
| Jump sections | Weak jumpers do not block the whole party |
| Triggering a skill check | The best-suited character performs the interaction |

## 🎲 Prepare Skill Checks

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⏱️

For important skill checks, consider swapping party members instead of immediately reloading. Relevant proficiencies, higher attributes, **Enhance Ability**, and **Bardic Inspiration** can make the difference.

### ✅ Check Before Reloading

If a skill check seems important, do not reload automatically. Often there are better options:

- return to camp and bring a specialist into the active party,
- move the right party member forward,
- prepare **Enhance Ability**,
- use **Bardic Inspiration**,
- equip items with relevant bonuses,
- split the party so the correct character interacts.

This is especially useful for Strength-heavy checks: a high-Athletics character with **Enhance Ability: Bull's Strength** and **Bardic Inspiration** can solve checks that the current party failed.

Related terms are summarized in [[15_Appendix#^glossary-spells|Glossary: Spells, Buffs, and Magical Effects]].

## 🗣️ Use Dialogue Controls

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⏱️

The dialogue interface has useful controls that are easy to miss. When one character is locked in conversation, the rest of the party may still be able to move, trade, or prepare.

| Control | Practical use |
|---|---|
| Character select | Move other party members before a fight starts or before a risky choice is made |
| Trade | Some NPCs can trade even when their normal dialogue does not advertise a shop |
| Dialogue history | Re-read missed lines, especially in multiplayer or when a scene was skipped |
| Attack | Treat as a deliberate hostile choice; do not press it unless you intentionally want to break the conversation |

Before choosing an obviously hostile dialogue option, check whether the party is standing in a bad cluster. If other characters can still move, split and position them first.

Source video: [Baldur's Gate 3: 20+ Easily Missed Tips (Spoiler Free) - Proxy Gate Tactician](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BGRu99XLSRc&t=277s).

## 🧪 Use the Alchemy Interface Deliberately

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ 💰 🧪

In the alchemy window, you can choose which **Any** ingredient is consumed. Hovering over ingredients shows which recipes still need them.

These two interface features prevent rare ingredients from being silently consumed by simple recipes. See [Alchemy](05_Alchemy.md) for details.

## 🧹 Keep the Action Bar Clean

**Markers:** ⭐ ⏱️

Weapon actions do not need to stay on the main action bar. They can be removed and accessed through the weapon menu when needed.

This reduces action bar clutter without removing options. See [Inventory and Equipment](04_Inventory_and_Equipment.md).

## ⏸️ Use Turn-Based Mode Outside Combat

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⏱️ 🎯

Turn-Based Mode is not only for fights. It is useful when timing matters but combat has not started yet.

Use it for:

- sneaking through vision cones,
- crossing traps or dangerous surfaces,
- positioning the party before triggering an encounter,
- controlling one character while the rest of the party stays still.

Additional source video: [I Wish I Knew These Tips Before Starting Baldur's Gate 3 - Jolsn](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lJCwezyHNlA).

## 🔔 Configure Reaction Prompts

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

Check important reactions and set them to ask before firing automatically. This prevents wasting strong reactions on low-value triggers.

This matters most for reactions that consume limited resources, interrupt enemy actions, or change positioning. Review these settings after major level-ups, because new class features can add new reactions.

Primary source video: [20 Things You STILL Aren't Doing In Baldur's Gate 3 - Doms Roundtable](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8PJgZ91My4o).

## ✅ Short Checklist Before Hard Fights

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️

| Step | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Inspect enemies | Identify resistances, immunities, and reactions |
| Read initiative | Prioritize dangerous enemies that act early |
| Split the party | Secure better starting positions |
| Show vision cones | Avoid unwanted detection |
| Distribute consumables | Make healing, bombs, and scrolls available |
