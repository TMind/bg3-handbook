# ⚔️ Combat System

Fights become easier when actions are not considered in isolation. Initiative, target priority, control, terrain, and consumables all interact.

> [!tip] Core habit
> Use the initiative bar as a threat list. A controlled enemy that loses its turn is often worth more than another damaged enemy that still acts.

## ⏱️ Use Initiative First

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️

The initiative bar is more than a turn order. Enemies that act before your characters are often the most important targets. Killing or controlling an enemy before its turn prevents damage, control spells, and positional pressure.

If an enemy will not reliably die in one round, conditions such as Hold, Sleep, Blindness, Prone, or other forms of crowd control are often more valuable than extra damage.

Combat terms used in this chapter are summarized in [[15_Appendix#^glossary-combat|Glossary: Combat and Build Terms]].

## 🎯 Target Priority

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️

Not every enemy is equally urgent. The best target order depends on danger and initiative.

| Target type | Priority |
|---|---|
| Enemy with an early turn | High, because it will otherwise act |
| Enemy with control effects | High, because lost turns are expensive |
| Caster concentrating on a strong spell | High, because breaking concentration can remove the effect |
| Heavily wounded enemy | High if a kill prevents its action |
| Single boss without adds | Weigh damage against control |
| Enemy that cannot act this round | Lower if other threats are active |

## 🧠 Damage or Control

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️ 🎯

Damage is good when it kills an enemy or puts it clearly within kill range. Control is better when the enemy would otherwise get a strong action.

Practical rule of thumb:

- If the enemy will safely die this round, focus damage.
- If it will not safely die, check control options.
- If it acts soon, prioritize a kill or control effect.
- If it has resistances, inspect first and switch damage type if needed.

## 💚 Pick Up Downed Allies

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

**Healing Word** is often better than **Help**, because it works at range and only costs a bonus action. The acting character can still attack, cast, or move.

**Help** remains useful when no healing option is available or when a character is already standing next to the downed ally. In many fights, **Healing Word** is the more efficient rescue because it combines range with strong action economy.

Help can also remove some adjacent ally problems such as being downed, prone, sleeping, or stuck in certain restraint effects. Use it when the ally's next turn is worth more than the helper's action.

If the ally only needs to be moved out of a harmful start-of-turn area, **Shove** can sometimes solve the problem with a bonus action instead. Check the direction carefully before pushing an ally.

## 🎒 Move Items During Combat

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⏱️ ⚔️ 🎯

Items can be moved between party members during combat. This is especially strong for healing potions, scrolls, bombs, arrows, and situational equipment.

This is free and works regardless of distance. It costs no action, so every character does not need to carry every resource. The important part is having enough relevant items before difficult fights.

Common immediate uses:

- a healing potion goes to a nearly defeated character,
- a scroll goes to the character with the available action,
- [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-hill-giant-strength\|Elixir of Hill Giant Strength]] is sent to the character who suddenly needs Strength,
- Scroll of Chain Lightning is sent to the character with the best line of sight,
- bombs or special arrows go to ranged characters,
- situational equipment is moved before it matters.

## 📦 Trigger Placed Items Deliberately

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️ 🎯

Moving an item in inventory is not the same as spending an action. In some fights, a placed potion, bomb, or small object can be triggered by an existing area effect or surface.

Use this only when it creates clear value:

- a potion can be placed where several allies can trigger it safely,
- a bomb can be placed into an existing damaging area,
- a dropped object can block or slow a narrow route,
- a door can be opened, closed, or used as a funnel around enemy movement.

The danger is self-inflicted damage. If a surface is involved, check the path and ally positions first. More environment examples are in [Using the Environment](03_Using_the_Environment.md).

Source video: [Baldur's Gate 3: 20+ Easily Missed Tips (Spoiler Free) - Proxy Gate Tactician](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BGRu99XLSRc&t=352s).

## 🕯️ Candle and Dip

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️ 🎯

A placed candle can be used to dip weapons. This adds fire damage and costs very few resources.

The advantage is small but very efficient early in the game, especially for characters with multiple attacks. It also works with ranged weapons, so archers can benefit too.

The extra damage is most attractive when there is no more important bonus-action or setup plan. Inspect enemies first if fire resistance may matter.

## 🎲 Avoid Disadvantage Before Spending Strong Attacks

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

Before using a high-value attack, check whether the character is attacking with disadvantage. Darkness, poor positioning, threatened ranged attacks, enemy conditions, or low ground can turn a good action into a likely miss.

Better habits:

- move before attacking if line of sight or range is bad,
- use high ground when it improves the shot,
- switch to a save-based spell if attack rolls are unreliable,
- spend Bless, Advantage, or setup on the character whose action matters most.

Source videos: [Baldur's Gate 3 Guide to Combat Mechanics - Fextralife](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BSLGMShwD-I), [Baldur's Gate 3 - Ultimate Beginner's Guide to Combat - WolfheartFPS](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9Q32QXyX4_Y).

## 🏃 Jump, Disengage, and Opportunity Attacks

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ ⚔️

Movement is part of the action economy. A character who runs away from a melee enemy can trigger an opportunity attack, so check whether **Disengage**, **Jump**, Shove, or control is safer.

Jump is especially useful when a normal path is too long, blocked, or covered by a surface. Strength elixirs improve jump distance and can turn bad positioning into a solvable problem.

Jump is often safer than walking out of surfaces such as ice, grease, fire, poison, or webs, because the character avoids pathing through the surface. It can also reach high ground before a ranged attack, improving accuracy when the new position has a better line and height.

Source videos: [Baldur's Gate 3 - 13 Combat Tips You NEED To Know - GameSpot](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DmHMXxcITJI), [I Wish I Knew These Tips Before Starting Baldur's Gate 3 - Jolsn](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lJCwezyHNlA).

## 💪 Throw and Shove

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️ 🎯

High Strength makes **Throw** and **Shove** much better. Enemies can be moved into chasms, hazardous surfaces, or off high positions.

Strength elixirs improve these tactics significantly. See [Elixirs and Potions](06_Elixirs_and_Potions.md).

For weapon throws, check whether the weapon has the **Thrown** tag. A thrown weapon with that tag uses its real weapon damage; random weapons without the tag are usually poor throw weapons.

World objects can also be thrown directly **at** enemies as improvised attacks: pick up a box, crate, chair, or similar object and throw it at a target for bludgeoning damage. Heavier objects hit harder, and the damage scales with Strength. This costs no ammunition or spell slots, so it is a solid fallback when a character has nothing better to do with an action, and it works with whatever clutter is already lying around the battlefield.

Throw range and throw value depend on Strength, object weight, and height difference. High ground improves thrown-object play, but do not build a whole fight around stacking crates unless it solves a specific encounter.

With very high Strength, Medium enemies can be thrown, not only Small enemies. **Throw** uses an action, while **Shove** uses a bonus action. If positioning is good, one strong character can potentially remove two enemies from a ledge in the same round.

Large or heavy enemies may be impossible to throw or shove effectively. If Shove is central to the plan, check size, weight, Strength, and positioning before spending setup resources.

### 💥 When Shove Is Strong

| Situation | Benefit |
|---|---|
| Enemy stands near an edge | Fall or chasm damage |
| Enemy blocks a chokepoint | Opens a path for your characters |
| Enemy has a strong position | Removes high ground or cover |
| Enemy stands near a hazardous surface | Pushes it into fire, oil, or another surface |
| Enemy is concentrating near danger | Can force movement and may break concentration |
| Ally is sleeping or standing in a harmful area | Can wake or move the ally if the direction is safe |

Source video: [Baldur's Gate 3 - Shoving Mechanics Explained - The Mages Corner](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GwPGrevjHrI).

## 🧱 Destroy the Environment

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️

Ladders, bridges, and walkways can simplify fights when enemies lose routes or fall. Chandeliers, barrels, oil, and explosives should be considered before and during fights.

## 🕊️ Remember Non-Lethal Attacks

**Markers:** ⭐ ⚠️

Non-lethal attacks are situational, but useful when a fight has moral, quest, or role-play consequences. Toggle them before the final blow, not after the target is already dead.

This is not a default combat setting. Use it deliberately, then turn it off again when normal lethal damage is expected.

Source video: [Baldur's Gate 3 - 13 Combat Tips You NEED To Know - GameSpot](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DmHMXxcITJI).

## 🍷 Buff Before Combat

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚔️ 🎯

Some consumables are especially strong before combat. **[[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^potion-of-speed\|Potion of Speed]]** can be thrown on the ground if several party members are standing close together. One potion can affect multiple characters.

Elixirs should be drunk directly instead. Thrown elixirs can last much shorter than expected or fail to work as intended. Details are in [Elixirs and Potions](06_Elixirs_and_Potions.md).

## ✅ Mini-Checklist Each Round

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️

| Question | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Which enemy acts next? | Set target priority |
| Will the target safely die? | Focus damage |
| Will it survive? | Check control options |
| Are there heights, edges, or objects? | Use Shove, Throw, or the environment |
| Is an ally downed? | Check Healing Word before Help |
