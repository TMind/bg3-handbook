# 🎒 Inventory and Equipment

Inventory management is not busywork. Good equipment and distributed consumables prevent lost turns, overloaded characters, and unnecessary reloads.

> [!tip] Core habit
> Resources only matter if the right character can use them at the right time. Spread healing, condition removal, scrolls, bombs, and arrows before hard fights.

## 🛡️ Read Proficiencies Carefully

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️

Armor proficiencies apply not only to chest armor, but also to helmets, gloves, and boots. Missing proficiency can therefore cause problems even through small-looking equipment pieces.

Before equipping new gear, check whether the character has the correct proficiency. This matters especially for:

- helmets,
- gloves,
- boots,
- armor pieces with strong individual bonuses.

A powerful effect is worth less if the equipment interferes with the character's core abilities.

### 🔎 Equipment Check After Looting

**Markers:** ⭐ ⚠️

After a major loot upgrade, do not equip everything immediately. Check the whole character sheet effect, not only the item tooltip.

| Check | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Proficiency | Prevents penalties from the wrong armor type |
| Role fit | A strong item on the wrong character can be wasted |
| Action economy | Bonus-action items compete with other bonus actions |
| Concentration and defense | Fragile casters may need survival more than damage |

This avoids the common mistake of equipping a powerful item that quietly weakens the character's main job.

## 🧲 Watch for Stat-Stick Gear

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️ 🎯

Some equipment is useful even when the character does not mainly attack with it. Treat these as **stat sticks**: items worn for passive bonuses, defensive value, or effects that apply beyond the item's own attack.

Common patterns:

- a shield on a proficient caster or ranged character for easy AC,
- a weapon that improves critical range or accuracy for more than its own attacks,
- a bow or melee weapon with a passive that also helps spells or the opposite weapon set,
- a utility item equipped briefly before a specific interaction.

After equipping this kind of item, check the character sheet or combat log instead of trusting the first tooltip impression. Some bonuses apply broadly; others only work with the item itself.

Source video: [Baldur's Gate 3: 20+ Easily Missed Tips (Spoiler Free) - Proxy Gate Tactician](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BGRu99XLSRc&t=405s).

## 🧹 Move Weapon Actions Off the Main Bar

**Markers:** ⭐ ⏱️

Weapon actions do not need to permanently fill the main action bar. They can be removed and accessed through the weapon menu when needed.

This keeps the action bar cleaner without losing options.

### 📌 What Should Stay on the Main Bar

| Category | Reason |
|---|---|
| Frequently used attacks | Fast access every round |
| Important bonus actions | Makes action economy easier to track |
| Healing and rescue options | Downed characters can be picked up faster |
| Control effects | More visible when an enemy will not reliably die |

Rare weapon actions can be moved to the weapon menu.

## 🧾 Use Bulk Inventory Shortcuts

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⏱️

Inventory management is much faster when items are selected in groups before sending, selling, or moving them. Use multi-select habits instead of dragging items one at a time.

Practical uses:

- move supplies and junk in batches,
- send heavy items to camp,
- mark vendor trash before opening the trade screen,
- distribute multiple consumables before a hard fight.

Primary source video: [20 Things You STILL Aren't Doing In Baldur's Gate 3 - Doms Roundtable](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8PJgZ91My4o).

### Fast Sorting Habits

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⏱️

Use the inventory search and sort tools together with multi-select:

- search for `scroll`, `arrow`, `potion`, or another item type,
- use range selection for whole groups,
- move the result into a dedicated bag or onto the character who uses it,
- mark vendor trash as wares before opening the trade screen.

This is faster than trying to clean the vendor window after the trade has already started.

## 🔁 Distribute Items During Combat

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⏱️ ⚔️ 🎯

Healing potions, scrolls, bombs, special arrows, and other consumables can be moved between party members during combat. This means every character does not need to carry everything personally.

For thrown potions, see [Elixirs and Potions](06_Elixirs_and_Potions.md).

### 🎯 Combat Access Matters More Than Storage

The party inventory can hold many resources, but fights are decided by who can use them at the right time. Before difficult encounters, move key consumables to characters that are likely to act early or stand in useful positions.

Good default distribution:

- healing on every character,
- stronger healing on frontline characters,
- scrolls on characters that can spare an action,
- special arrows on ranged attackers,
- bombs on characters with high ground or good throwing lines,
- condition removal spread across the party.

## ✅ Useful Distribution Before Hard Fights

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚔️

| Item | Who should have access? |
|---|---|
| Healing potions | Every character |
| Stronger healing potions | Frontline characters and fragile key characters |
| [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^remedial-potion\|Remedial Potions]] | Ideally every character in the endgame |
| Bombs | Characters with good positioning or available actions |
| Special arrows | Ranged characters |
| Scrolls | Characters that can realistically act in combat |

## 📜 Scrolls and One-Time Tools

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ 💰 ⚔️

Scrolls are strongest when they solve a specific problem without requiring prepared spells. They should not be treated only as vendor trash.

Use them when:

- the needed spell is not prepared,
- the normal caster is downed or out of range,
- a fight can be simplified immediately,
- spending a scroll saves a higher-value resource.

Before selling scrolls, keep at least the ones that cover gaps in the current party.

## 🧪 Do Not Treat Alchemy Ingredients as Junk

**Markers:** ⭐⭐⭐ ⚠️ 💰 🧪

Ingredients should not be sold indiscriminately. Many strong elixirs and potions depend on specific ingredients that do not reliably respawn.

Before selling rare ingredients, check [Alchemy](05_Alchemy.md).

## ⚖️ Solve Carry Weight Practically

**Markers:** ⭐ ⏱️

Strength elixirs increase not only combat power, but also carrying capacity. This is useful for characters carrying heavy loot, barrels, or many consumables.

Details on [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-hill-giant-strength\|Hill Giant Strength]] and [[06_Elixirs_and_Potions#^elixir-of-cloud-giant-strength\|Cloud Giant Strength]] are in [Elixirs and Potions](06_Elixirs_and_Potions.md).

## 🧺 Send Camp Supplies to Camp

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⏱️

Camp supplies are heavy and rarely need to stay in active inventories. Send them to camp regularly so the party has more carrying capacity for combat tools, loot, and quest items.

This is especially useful before long dungeon sections where returning to a vendor or camp chest is inconvenient.

If camp storage becomes cluttered, put a camp-supplies backpack inside the Traveller's Chest. New supplies sent to camp can then stay grouped instead of mixing with gear, scrolls, and quest clutter.

Primary source video: [20 Things You STILL Aren't Doing In Baldur's Gate 3 - Doms Roundtable](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8PJgZ91My4o). Additional source: [13 Things That are Actually Overpowered in Baldur's Gate 3 - Toyhouze](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1g8TJBjPfqw&t=151s).

## 🧰 Send Locked Chests to Camp

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⏱️ 🎯

If a locked chest is not urgent and the current party cannot open it cleanly, send it to camp. Open it later with the right Sleight of Hand character, gloves, buffs, or tools.

This is useful when:

- the area is dangerous and standing still is costly,
- the current character is not the lockpicking specialist,
- the party lacks the right buff or equipment,
- the chest is heavy and slows exploration.

Do not forget to process these later. Sending locked containers to camp is a deferred task, not a reason to ignore them forever.

Source: [13 Things That are Actually Overpowered in Baldur's Gate 3 - Toyhouze](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1g8TJBjPfqw&t=151s).

## 🧯 Do Not Overcentralize Resources

**Markers:** ⭐⭐ ⚠️

Putting every important item on one character creates risk. If that character is downed, controlled, isolated, or too far away, the party may lose access to key tools.

Better pattern:

| Resource | Distribution |
|---|---|
| Basic healing | Every character |
| Strong healing | At least two characters |
| Condition removal | Spread across the party |
| Bombs and arrows | Characters with good delivery options |
| Utility scrolls | Characters with spare actions or strong positioning |

This keeps the party functional even after a bad opening round.

## ✅ Short Inventory Checklist

**Markers:** ⭐⭐

| Timing | Check |
|---|---|
| Before a hard fight | Distribute healing, control, bombs, and scrolls |
| After changing equipment | Check proficiency for every piece |
| Before selling | Set aside alchemy ingredients and rare consumables |
| After a Long Rest | Recheck vendors and refill resources |
