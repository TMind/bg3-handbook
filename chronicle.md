# Party Chronicle

> ⚠️ **Spoiler:** this is the story of the party's run so far, told from their own journey through the campaign.
>
> Handles only: **Kao** (Conjuration Wizard, party lead) and **TMind** (Death Domain Cleric) are the two player characters; the rest are their companions.

**Sources.** The *events* come from the current save's quest journal — the full record of every quest completed on this run — plus the campaign notes. The *timeline* is anchored to the **136 named location-saves** of this playthrough (out of 233 save folders on disk), spanning from the crash at **1h 24m** to **Wyrm's Rock Fortress at 100h 29m**. Beats below are tagged with the save that marks them, like *(Emerald Grove — 9h 24m)*.

## Prologue — The Worm and the Wreck

It began the way every nightmare on the Sword Coast seems to: with a mind flayer's tadpole pushed in behind the eye. Kao and TMind woke aboard a nautiloid falling out of the Hells, ceremorphosis already ticking in their skulls, and fought their way off the ship as it tore itself apart. They came down hard on the coast *(Ravaged Beach — 1h 24m)* — strangers thrown together by a shared death sentence.

Two others clawed free of the same disaster. **Lae'zel**, a githyanki warrior, had fought beside them on the ship; they found her again on the far side of the crash, locked in a tiefling cage, and cut her loose. **Shadowheart** they pried out of an intact nautiloid pod with an Eldritch Rune. The four of them started walking — not as friends, but as people with the same worm and the same clock.

The one thing they agreed on: **find a cure before the tadpole turns them.**

## The Road — Act by Act

The two player characters carry the main thread. This is the spine of the run, and it maps cleanly onto the saves.

### Act 1 — The Search for a Cure *(≈1h–45h)*

They reached a walled druid grove full of refugees and found their first hope there *(Emerald Grove — 9h 24m)* — and lost it fast. Every lead curdled. The grove healer **Nettie** died before she could help. The swamp "grandmother," **Auntie Ethel**, turned out to be a hag and turned violent *(Sunlit Wetlands — 17h 36m)*. Past the **Blighted Village** *(13h 27m)* and into goblin territory *(Shattered Sanctum — 21h–23h; Goblin Camp — 33h 59m)*, the priestess **Gut** offered her chapel and then drugged and caged them, hoping to watch them transform.

The grove itself fared no better. The one healer everyone swore by — the Archdruid **Halsin**, lost bear-shaped in goblin hands — was never found alive: he died before he could help anyone, and the conflict around the grove ended in blood. **Kagha** fell early, **Zevlor** died, the surviving tieflings scattered, and the druids were defeated. Whatever hope Act 1 held, it did not survive it.

Underground the search widened *(Underdark — 24h 18m; Ebonlake Grotto — 25h; Arcane Tower — 30h 52m)*, and the one genuinely friendly face was a mind flayer: **Omeluum** couldn't pull the parasite, but its experiments cracked something open — a strange new power in the worm — and left them a **Ring of Mind-Shielding** to keep it leashed. Through the ancient forge *(Grymforge — 40h–44h)* they kept moving, still uncured, but now certain of one thing: the **artefact** they carried mattered. The goblin king coveted it; the githyanki hunted it without pause.

The trail led up a mountain to the githyanki *(Crèche Yllek — 46h–50h)*. In the **Inquisitor's Chamber** *(48h 18m)* it was finally named — the **Astral Prism** — and the lich-queen **Vlaakith** herself, appearing as a projection, ordered them to enter it and kill whoever was imprisoned inside. They stepped in. They found a being there, spoke with it, and chose *not* to kill it. It told them they were on the right track and pointed them at **Moonrise Towers**.

### Act 2 — The Shadow-Cursed Lands *(≈45h–82h)*

Into the dark *(Shadow-Cursed Lands — 45h 09m)*, where the sunlight itself was gone and the only safe ground was a besieged inn *(Last Light Inn — 55h 07m)*. They pushed through the horrors of a corrupted hospital *(House of Healing / Morgue — 59h–62h)* and down into the trial-vaults of the goddess of loss *(Gauntlet of Shar — 63h–68h)*, chasing the Nightsong to its cage *(Nightsong's Prison — 69h 28m)*.

One door stayed closed. They woke the sleeping Flaming Fist **Art Cullagh** *(Isobel's Room — 55h 19m)*, who carried the key to the land's healing — the spirit **Thaniel**, lost in the Shadowfell. But Thaniel's rescue needed the one druid who could reach him, and Halsin was long dead. Art despaired, and the party moved on: the **shadow curse was never lifted** on this run.

The road to the enemy's seat ran through a haunted town *(Reithwin Tollhouse — 70h–71h; Sharran Sanctuary — 72h)* and up into the tower itself *(Moonrise Towers — 73h–80h)*. Beneath it they found the horror the Absolute was built on — a mind flayer breeding operation *(Necrotic Laboratory — 75h 47m; Tadpoling Centre — 76h 54m; Mind Flayer Colony — 77h–78h)* — and the general commanding it all, **Ketheric Thorm**. They broke him, and took the first of the three **Netherstones**.

Their strange guide had a face by now. The being from the Prism — the **Dream Visitor** — revealed itself in the deep astral space *(Astral Plane — 82h 36m)* as the **Emperor**, a rogue illithid, and it has been riding in their heads ever since, promising the tadpoles are the *only* way to fight what's coming.

### Act 3 — Baldur's Gate *(≈82h–100h)*

Down the mountain and into the city's outskirts *(Wyrms Lookout — 81h–83h; Wyrms Crossing — 83h 17m)*, through the tents of a travelling **Circus of the Last Days** *(84h 53m)* and the market district *(Rivington — 91h–96h)*. Here the true enemy finally stood clear: the Absolute is an **elder brain** — a Netherbrain — kept on a leash by three **Chosen of the Dead Three**. To seize it, the party needs all three Netherstones. They hold Ketheric's. Two remain.

And here the companions' own reckonings all come due at once — Cazador's palace, Raphael's House of Hope, the wizard of Waterdeep's bargain — while the party works toward the fortress at the heart of it all *(Wyrm's Rock Fortress — 99h–100h; Wyrm's Rock Prison — 99h 55m)*, carrying the Emperor's unanswered question: once the brain is theirs to command — **liberate, or conquer?**

TMind fights this war as a **Death Domain cleric**, anchoring the line with Spirit Guardians, Warding Bond, and a healer's kit that keeps everyone upright; Kao is the **Conjuration control-mage** who decides where the enemy is *allowed* to be. Between them they turned a death sentence into a campaign.

## Lae'zel — The Warrior Who Chose

Lae'zel joined with total certainty *(Emerald Grove — 9h 24m)*: take the infected to a Githyanki Crèche, submit to the purification device, and be cleansed. Certainty was the first thing the road took from her.

The crèche was real *(Crèche Yllek — 46h–50h)*, tucked in the ruins of a mountain monastery *(Rosymorn Monastery — 52h 19m)* — but the "cure," the zaith'isk, nearly killed them; their parasites were too unusual for it. Worse was the throne room *(Inquisitor's Chamber — 48h 18m)*. **Vlaakith**, her venerable queen, appeared and ordered her to enter the Astral Prism and murder the prisoner inside. Lae'zel urged the party to obey. They refused — and when the dust settled, **Kith'rak Voss** told her the truth she'd been raised never to hear: purification and ascension are lies, and Vlaakith devours her own faithful.

What broke her also freed her. Lae'zel turned traitor to a tyrant and found a cause instead: the prisoner in the Prism is **Prince Orpheus**, Gith's own son, the key to liberating her people. Vlaakith came to camp herself and offered to make her Chosen if she'd only kill Orpheus. She refused, to the queen's face. Now her road runs toward Raphael's House of Hope and the **Orphic Hammer** — with the devil circling, offering to sell that same freedom for a price the party already turned down.

**Build:** Battle Master Fighter, level 10, GWM. The frontline pin and damage engine — Trip, push, and hold the kill zone.

## Astarion — The Pale Elf

Astarion travelled a long way on a lie of omission. He was the strange, hostile elf who joined once he learned the others were infected too, and the party found out what he was the first night one of them woke to him drinking their blood. They let him feed enough to keep his strength and set a rule he mostly keeps: enemies are fair game, innocents are not.

The past came hunting. A monster hunter, **Gandrel**, was sent to drag him back to Baldur's Gate — sent, Astarion suspected, by his master. That master is **Cazador Szarr**, the vampire lord who had him murdered by a Gur nomad two centuries ago and raised him as a spawn and a slave, unable to refuse an order. It was a devil who filled in the rest: near the Thorm mausoleum *(House of Healing — 59h–62h)*, **Raphael** offered Astarion the truth about his scars in exchange for killing an orthon — and then told it. Cazador plans the **Rite of Profane Ascension**, sacrificing his spawn for terrible power, and Astarion is the missing piece he needs back.

So he stopped running. In the city he met **Ulma** and her tribe of monster hunters — whom he once betrayed for Cazador — and took the chance to make it right: help their stolen children, and end Cazador for good. His road now points straight at the palace and a confrontation two centuries in the making.

**Build:** Thief Rogue, level 10. Scout, lockpick, and precision damage — the one who starts the fight and deletes the soft targets.

## Shadowheart — Shar's Daughter, Selûne's Choice

Shadowheart came out of that nautiloid pod with a locked memory and a devotion to the Lady of Loss. Her road was a quest for the **Nightsong** — a relic, everyone assumed, that Shar's faithful had hidden and that a wizard in Baldur's Gate would pay a fortune to obtain.

The trail ran from a puzzle in the Goblin Camp to a secret Temple of Shar under the Thorm mausoleum, through the goddess's own trial-vaults *(Gauntlet of Shar — 63h–68h)*, with Ketheric's servant **Balthazar** racing her for the prize — and finally into the **Shadowfell** itself *(Nightsong's Prison — 69h 28m)*. There the assumption shattered: the Nightsong is not an artefact but a living woman, caged in the dark. Handed the order to strike her down, **Shadowheart spared her** — and in that moment turned away from Shar and toward **Selûne**, the moon and her mother's true faith. The Nightsong walked out of the Shadowfell and into their camp.

*(She rides the bench in the current lineup, but she's the best camp cleric on the roster — Aid, Death Ward, Warding Bond — and the reason those buffs stay topped up.)*

## Gale — The Wizard of Waterdeep

Gale claims to have been on the nautiloid too, which means he's infected like the rest — but that's the *smaller* of his problems. Buried in his chest is an orb of corrupted Weave that hungers, and the only thing that quiets it is feeding it magic: he consumes the Weave from potent magical items, and each dose buys a little less time than the last. The unspoken warning under all his charm is that if it isn't fed, it detonates — and not quietly.

Then his old teacher came calling. **Elminster** found him with a message from **Mystra** herself: find the **Heart of the Absolute** and destroy it by detonating the orb — a walking bomb aimed at the one enemy big enough to matter. Elminster stabilised the orb long enough to make that a *choice* rather than a countdown. Gale carries that offer, and its terrible arithmetic, in reserve.

*(Also a camp companion in the current lineup — the backup Wizard for when AoE beats stealth.)*

## Where They Stand

The cure they went looking for in Act 1 was never coming; the worm is part of the plan now. What started as four strangers with a shared death sentence has become a party with a war to finish — two Netherstones still to take, three Chosen to face, and the Netherbrain waiting at the heart of Baldur's Gate. They're at Wyrm's Rock, level 10, geared and buffed, standing at the edge of the endgame with the Emperor's question still open.

Liberate, or conquer. That part of the story is still theirs to write.
