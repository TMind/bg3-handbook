# Party Chronicle

> ⚠️ **Spoiler:** this is the story of the party's run so far, told from their own journey through the campaign. Drawn from the quest journal and the campaign notes.
>
> Handles only: **Kao** (Conjuration Wizard, party lead) and **TMind** (Death Domain Cleric) are the two player characters; the rest are their companions.

*Currently at Wyrm's Rock Fortress in Baldur's Gate — roughly 100 hours in, the whole party at level 10, closing on the endgame.*

---

## Prologue — The Worm and the Wreck

It began the way every nightmare in the Sword Coast seems to: with a mind flayer's tadpole pushed behind the eye. Kao and TMind woke aboard a nautiloid falling out of the Hells, ceremorphosis already ticking in their skulls. They fought their way through the crash and came down hard on the Material Plane, strangers thrown together by a shared death sentence.

Two others clawed free of the same wreck and its aftermath. **Lae'zel**, a githyanki warrior, had helped them escape the ship; they found her again on the far side of the crash, locked in a tiefling cage, and cut her loose. **Shadowheart** they pried out of an intact pod with an Eldritch Rune. The four of them started walking — not as friends, but as people with the same worm and the same clock.

The one thing they agreed on was the goal: **find a cure before the tadpole turns them.**

## Kao & TMind — The Hunt for a Cure

The two player characters carry the main thread, and Act 1 was one long, failing search for a healer.

Every promising lead curdled. A healer named **Nettie** in the Emerald Grove died before she could help. The swamp "grandmother," **Auntie Ethel**, turned out to be a hag and turned violent. The goblins' **Priestess Gut** offered her chapel and then drugged and caged them, hoping to watch them transform. The druid **Halsin** was lost to the grove's own crisis. In the Underdark, a genuinely friendly mind flayer, **Omeluum**, couldn't pull the parasite either — but its experiments cracked something open, unlocking a strange new power in the worm and leaving them a Ring of Mind-Shielding to keep it leashed.

What kept them moving was the thing they were carrying: an artefact the goblin king Dror Ragzlin coveted and the githyanki hunted relentlessly. At the crèche the inquisitor named it — the **Astral Prism** — and the lich-queen Vlaakith herself commanded them to step inside and kill whoever was imprisoned within. They entered. They found a being there, spoke to it, and chose *not* to kill it. It told them they were on the right track and pointed them at **Moonrise Towers**.

That being — their "Dream Visitor," later revealed as the **Emperor**, a rogue illithid — became the strange guide riding shotgun in their heads for the rest of the road: through the shadow-cursed lands, past **Ketheric Thorm**, and into Baldur's Gate, where the shape of the real enemy finally showed itself. The Absolute is an **elder brain**, a Netherbrain, kept on a leash by three Chosen of the Dead Three. To face it, the party needs the **Netherstones**. They already hold Ketheric's. The question the Emperor left them with is the one they still carry into the fortress: once the brain is theirs to command — **liberate, or conquer?**

TMind fights this war as a **Death Domain cleric**, anchoring the line with Spirit Guardians, Warding Bond, and a healer's kit that keeps everyone standing; Kao is the **Conjuration control-mage** who decides where the enemy is *allowed* to be. Between them they turned a death sentence into a campaign.

## Lae'zel — The Warrior Who Chose

Lae'zel joined believing with total certainty: take the infected to a Githyanki Crèche, submit to the purification device, and be cleansed. Certainty was the first thing the road took from her.

The crèche was real — hidden in the ruins of Rosymorn Monastery — but the "cure," the zaith'isk, nearly killed them; their parasites were too unusual for it. Worse was the throne room. **Vlaakith**, her venerable queen, appeared and ordered her to enter the Astral Prism and murder the prisoner inside. Lae'zel urged the party to obey. They refused — and when the dust settled, **Kith'rak Voss** told her the truth she'd been raised to never hear: purification and ascension are lies. Vlaakith devours her own faithful.

What broke also freed her. Lae'zel turned traitor to a tyrant and found a cause instead: the prisoner in the Prism is **Prince Orpheus**, Gith's own son, the key to liberating her people. Vlaakith came to camp and offered to make her Chosen if she'd only kill Orpheus. She refused to the queen's face. Now her road runs toward the House of Hope and the **Orphic Hammer** — with Raphael circling, offering to sell the same freedom for a devil's price the party already turned down.

**Build:** Battle Master Fighter, level 10, GWM. The party's frontline pin and damage engine — Trip, push, and hold the kill zone.

## Astarion — The Pale Elf

Astarion travelled a long way on a lie of omission. He was the strange, hostile elf who joined once he learned the others were infected too — and the party found out what he was the first night one of them woke to him drinking their blood. They let him feed enough to keep his strength, and set a rule he mostly keeps: enemies are fair game, innocents are not.

The past came hunting. A monster hunter, **Gandrel**, was sent to drag him back to Baldur's Gate — sent, Astarion suspected, by his master. That master is **Cazador Szarr**, the vampire lord who murdered Astarion by a Gur nomad's hand two centuries ago and raised him as a spawn and a slave, unable to refuse an order. Through Raphael the party learned *why* Cazador wants him back: the **Rite of Profane Ascension**, a ritual that sacrifices his spawn for terrible power — and Astarion is the missing piece.

So he stopped running. In the city he met **Ulma** and her monster hunters, whom he once betrayed for Cazador, and took the chance to make it right: help their stolen children, and end Cazador for good. His road now points straight at the palace and the confrontation two hundred years in the making.

**Build:** Thief Rogue, level 10. Scout, lockpick, and precision damage — the one who starts the fight and deletes the soft targets.

## Shadowheart — Shar's Daughter, Selûne's Choice

Shadowheart came out of that nautiloid pod with a locked memory and a devotion to the Lady of Loss. Her road was a quest for the **Nightsong** — a relic, everyone assumed, that Shar's faithful had hidden away and that a wizard in Baldur's Gate would pay a fortune to obtain.

The trail ran from a puzzle under the Goblin Camp to a secret Temple of Shar beneath the Thorm Mausoleum, with Ketheric's servant **Balthazar** racing them for the prize, and finally into the **Shadowfell** itself. There the assumption shattered: the Nightsong is not an artefact but a living woman, caged in the dark. Faced with the order to strike her down, **Shadowheart spared her** — and in that moment turned away from Shar and toward **Selûne**, the moon and her mother's true faith. The Nightsong walked out of the Shadowfell and into their camp.

*(In the current party she rides the bench, but she's the best camp cleric on the roster — Aid, Death Ward, Warding Bond — and the reason those buffs stay topped up.)*

## Gale — The Wizard of Waterdeep

Gale claims to have been on the nautiloid too, which means he's infected like the rest — but that's the *smaller* of his problems. Buried in his chest is an orb of corrupted Weave that hungers, and the only thing that quiets it is feeding it magic: he consumes the Weave out of potent magical items, and each dose buys a little less time than the last. The unspoken warning under his charm is that if it isn't fed, it detonates.

Then his old teacher came calling. **Elminster** found him with a message from **Mystra** herself: find the **Heart of the Absolute** and destroy it by detonating the orb — a walking bomb aimed at the one enemy big enough to matter. Elminster stabilised the orb long enough to make that a choice rather than a countdown. Gale carries that offer, and its terrible arithmetic, in reserve.

*(Also a camp companion in the current lineup — the backup Wizard when AoE beats stealth.)*

## Where They Stand

The cure they went looking for in Act 1 was never coming; the worm is part of the plan now. What started as four strangers with a shared death sentence has become a party with a war to finish — Netherstones to gather, three Chosen to face, and the Netherbrain waiting in the heart of Baldur's Gate. They're at Wyrm's Rock, level 10, geared and buffed, standing at the edge of the endgame with the Emperor's question still unanswered.

Liberate, or conquer. That part of the story is still theirs to write.
