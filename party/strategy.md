# Party Strategy — Kao, Lae'zel, Stefan, Astarion, Shadowheart

## Core Concept: The Aura Blender + Radiant Pressure
The party locks enemies inside Stefan's Spirit Guardians aura and grinds them down. Shadowheart adds a second damage axis (radiant/fire) and support depth, especially strong against Act 2 undead.

```
Kao (control) → Lae'zel (pin) → Stefan (aura) → Astarion (execute)
                                               ↑
                              Shadowheart (radiant offense + support)
```

## Standard Fight Opener

### Before combat
1. Stefan casts **Spirit Guardians** out of enemy line-of-sight (timer doesn't tick outside combat)
2. Pre-cast **Bless** or other round-based buffs the same way
3. Astarion moves into **stealth position**
4. Kao identifies where to place control spells

### Turn 1
1. **Astarion** opens from stealth with Sneak Attack — or Kao drops **Web** ahead of enemies
2. **Lae'zel** jumps/charges into the enemy center — uses **Trip Attack** on a key target (prone = stuck in aura + Advantage for allies)
3. **Stefan** moves in with Spirit Guardians active — walks into the same cluster as Lae'zel
4. **Kao** drops **Web** or **Hold Person** on the group

### Turn 2+ (the blender)
- Enemies are: slowed (Spirit Guardians), stuck (Web/prone), and unable to reach the backline
- Stefan stays in the middle — aura ticks every turn on every enemy nearby
- Lae'zel uses **Sentinel** (when taken) to stop any enemy that tries to leave
- Astarion picks off prone or weakened targets with Sneak Attack
- Kao refreshes control as needed or switches to Fireball on grouped enemies

## Spell Placement Rules
- **Place control ON enemies**, not between your party and the enemies
- Lae'zel can **Jump** over Web/Grease — she doesn't need to walk through control zones
- **Edge engagement**: Lae'zel stands at the edge of a Web, not inside it
- Drop AoE (Fireball, etc.) *behind* the enemy line, not on top of Lae'zel + Stefan
- Safe control spells (no friendly fire risk): Web, Hold Person, Slow, Banishment

## Healing Potions
- **Throw** potions at downed allies' feet — splash heals and works at range
- **Direct use** (right-click → Use on ally) requires melee range but is precise
- Stefan uses **Healing Word** (bonus action) to pick up downed allies without losing his turn

## Spirit Guardians Efficiency
- Don't cast it for small fights — save for groups, elites, or enclosed encounters
- Fight in chokepoints; don't spread out
- One strong cast > multiple wasteful casts
- Short Rest after a Spirit Guardians fight to recover HP

## Shadowheart's Role

### Positioning
- Backline, stays out of melee — Shadowheart is not a frontliner
- Pairs with Kao in the backline; Stefan and Lae'zel own the frontline

### Turn Priority (in order)
1. **Spiritual Weapon** (bonus action, first turn) — free damage every subsequent turn
2. **Fireball / Spirit Guardians** if enemies are clustered and Stefan's aura doesn't cover them
3. **Radiance of the Dawn** (Channel Divinity) — strong AoE vs. undead, no concentration
4. **Healing Word** (bonus action) — pick up downed allies without burning her action
5. **Warding Flare** (reaction) — use when a high-damage enemy targets Shadowheart or a squishy ally

### Concentration Management
- Shadowheart should own **Bless** — frees Stefan to hold Spirit Guardians
- If Bless is already running, her next best concentration option is **Spirit Guardians** (separate cluster) or **Flaming Sphere**
- Never compete with Kao's concentration (Web, Hold Person)

### Undead Encounters (Act 2 priority)
- Open with **Radiance of the Dawn** — hits all undead in range, no concentration cost
- Follow with **Fireball** or **Scorching Ray** for burst
- **Spiritual Weapon** still goes up turn 1 as bonus action

## When to Swap Party Members
- **Swap Astarion → Gale** if you need more AoE and less stealth utility
- **Keep Lae'zel** — she is the cornerstone of the aura blender when available. Without her, Stefan gets overwhelmed and the backline is exposed.

## Current Active Party (Temple of Shar)
**Kao / Astarion / Shadowheart / Stefan** — Lae'zel benched for storyline.

### Tactical adjustments without Lae'zel
- **Kao's Web is now the primary pin** — must land before Stefan enters melee
- **No prone setup** — Astarion loses the Trip Attack crit chain; needs an ally within 5ft or Greater Invisibility for Sneak Attack
- **Stefan is the only frontliner** — don't let him get surrounded; use chokepoints more aggressively
- **Shadowheart's Bless** frees Stefan to hold Spirit Guardians concentration — use it every meaningful fight
- Two clerics = deep healing reserves; don't be stingy with spell slots

## Key Synergy Interactions
| Setup | Effect |
|---|---|
| Lae'zel Trip Attack + Stefan aura | Enemy prone, can't move, takes aura damage |
| Kao Web + Stefan Spirit Guardians | Enemy truly cannot escape, takes both damage sources |
| Lae'zel Sentinel (when taken) + Web | Enemies physically stopped from leaving the kill zone |
| Greater Invisibility on Astarion | Advantage every turn = guaranteed Sneak Attack |
| Gale Evocation + Lae'zel melee | Safe AoE blasting right on top of frontline |
| Shadowheart Bless + Stefan Spirit Guardians | Bless boosts Lae'zel/Astarion attacks; Stefan holds aura — no concentration conflict |
| Shadowheart Spiritual Weapon + Shadowheart action | Two attacks per turn from one character — highly efficient |
| Shadowheart Radiance of the Dawn + Stefan aura | Undead take radiant burst + ongoing aura damage in same turn |
| Shadowheart Silence on caster + Lae'zel engage | Enemy caster locked down, Lae'zel closes in freely |
